## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [See Selectively, Act Adaptively: Dual-Level Structural Decomposition for Bimanual Robot Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13279v1) — Choi, Son, Lim · arXiv Jun 2026
  Proposes a VLA framework with a View-Selective Visual Router that dynamically adjusts wrist-view contributions based on task stage and context, plus an Interaction-Aware Action MoE that decomposes bimanual action generation. +27.7% simulation and +43.3% real-world over a monolithic VLA baseline. This is surf's core claim — that selective visual processing conditioned on task context is necessary, not just helpful — validated in a controlled VLA experiment. The View-Selective Visual Router is a view-level instantiation of surf's patch-level selector; the task-stage dependence directly corroborates the step-wise language sensitivity finding from 6/11 (When Does Language Matter). Note: Young-Chae Son is a listed author — may be connected to @sonSelectivePerceptionRobot2026 in the reading list.

- [Learnable Token Sparsification for Efficient Gigapixel Whole Slide Image Reasoning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/b5b72d246c868c112fc99081339e2d76a7760d74) — Chen et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Reformulates token reduction as a trainable sparsification problem using a SparseLearn module: variance-preserving noise gate + differentiable Soft Top-K during training, deterministic Hard Top-K at inference (SparseLearn discarded). Achieves 32/~4096 tokens (0.78% of original sequence) with 73% accuracy on a VLM benchmark, outperforming heuristic baselines. The training-inference disentanglement design directly solves surf's [[experiments/contrastive alignment]] implementation problem: how to backpropagate through a hard token selection decision. The SparseLearn architecture is a plug-in training wrapper over any scorer — composable with surf's contrastive patch scorer.

- [RepWAM: World Action Modeling with Representation Visual-Action Tokenizers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13674v1) — arXiv Jun 2026
  Argues that existing world action models inherit reconstruction-oriented video tokenizers, which preserve visual fidelity but provide limited guidance for instruction-following dynamics. Proposes semantic visual-action tokenizers that learn representations grounded in action prediction rather than pixel reconstruction. Directly relevant to surf's contrastive alignment design: RepWAM provides external evidence that reconstruction-quality objectives are insufficient for action-task visual representations — surf's contrastive (task-conditioned) patch scoring addresses exactly this failure mode. Read alongside ReconVLA (6/10) and AdaTok (6/9): all three converge on the same message from different directions.

**adjacent**
- [WEAVER: An Effective World Model for Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13672v1) — Jain, Wu, Farebrother, Swamy, Bajcsy · arXiv Jun 2026
  Multi-view world model trained on flow-matching loss; achieves ρ=0.870 correlation between simulated and real success rate, 38% policy improvement over π0.5 with WEAVER-based policy improvement, 14% test-time planning improvement at 5–10× speedup over prior WMs. Adjacent: if surf's selective token experiments can be evaluated via WEAVER rollouts before real-robot testing, iteration cost drops substantially. WEAVER's multi-view design also means surf's spatial token selection operates at the same granularity as WEAVER's per-view representation — composable pipeline.

- [Two Bridges, One Pathway: From VLMs to Generalizable VLAs with Embodied Trajectory-Coupled Data](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/22bdfa15f0bce91b82a4d5bd593c3ff0c2ae3fca) — Yin et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Proposes ETC data (embodied trajectory-coupled supervision — vision-language labels derived from robot scenes, not action labels) as a middle stage between VLM pretraining and VLA fine-tuning. Three-stage training: distribution bridging → objective bridging → retentive adaptation. Adjacent to surf's training design: ETC data is exactly what surf's contrastive alignment could produce — task-relevant vision-language annotations from robot trajectories without requiring action labels. The "distribution bridging" stage is what surf's contrastive pre-training step approximates.

- [SPARC: Reliable Spatial Annotations from Robot Demonstrations at Scale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13497v1) — arXiv Jun 2026
  Framework for auto-labeling robot demos with bounding boxes, object trajectories, and manipulation phase labels, each with a reliability calibration score. Adjacent to surf's training data problem: SPARC provides a scalable pipeline for generating the spatial supervision signal (which patches contain task-relevant objects) that surf's contrastive selector needs for ground-truth scoring. If SPARC's phase labels correlate with selective visual attention, they provide a training-time proxy for "which patches matter at this step."

**horizon**
- [Budget-Constrained Step-Level Diffusion Caching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13496v1) — arXiv Jun 2026
  Proposes BudCache: inverts threshold-based diffusion caching (quality→latency) to budget-constrained allocation (latency→quality). Casts per-step cache decisions as a sequential knapsack problem and solves it with a global policy that distributes a fixed compute budget across denoising steps. Horizon: surf's iterative selector is the same class of problem — allocate a fixed visual token budget across selection passes, where each pass has a cost and a marginal quality gain. BudCache's global budget policy is a direct architectural template for surf's efficiency/RL module when compute allocation is the objective.

- [Dense Supervision, Sparse Updates: On the Sparsity and Geometry of On-Policy Distillation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13657v1) — arXiv Jun 2026
  Analyzes on-policy distillation (OPD) across language and VL model pairs; finds that despite dense teacher supervision, parameter updates are sparse and occupy a persistent "active subspace." Horizon: surf's contrastive alignment will fine-tune a pretrained VLM backbone. If contrastive training similarly activates a sparse, geometrically coherent subspace, it suggests low-rank adaptation (LoRA) with a modest rank may capture the full contrastive update — informing the LoRA rank choice in the spec's architecture section.
