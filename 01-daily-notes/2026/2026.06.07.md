### digest (auto)

**core**
- [Look, Zoom, Understand: The Robotic Eyeball for Embodied Perception](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/2c0375bf1aaa5637453373a5bd9b267bfdaab26d) — Yang et al. · arXiv
  Formulates language-guided active visual perception as deciding where to look and at what scale under pixel/spatial budget constraints — directly formalizes surf's problem structure. Their budget-constrained task-relevant query formulation is the closest embodied-AI analog to surf's patch selection setup seen in this digest stream. Directly relevant to [[questions]] active perception thread and the Tianqin setup comparison.

- [DepthCache: Depth-Guided Training-Free Visual Token Merging for VLA Model Inference](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/349a9a94f5d47624a65a84e9125fd430f18f6fea) — Li et al. · arXiv
  Uses depth as a structural prior to partition VLA observations into depth-stratified regions, then applies selective merging that preserves spatial structure for manipulation. Training-free. Direct comparison to surf's line-guided approach: depth captures geometry cheaply without learned supervision, as line drawings do. The key question: does depth-stratified merging preserve action-relevant contact/affordance patches better or worse than line-guided selection? This is a baseline [[claims/structure helps]] must address.

- [Look, Focus, Act: Efficient and Robust Robot Learning via Human Gaze and Foveated Vision Transformers](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/8f7985729b7c0f0f365839dcfcf0474d5a9f6c01) — Chuang et al. · arXiv
  GIAVA: uses continuous human eye-tracking to provide gaze-directed foveated high-resolution views in a robot learning system. Directly relevant to surf's DAA gaze supervision question — GIAVA's dense continuous gaze defines the upper bound for gaze-supervised patch selection that surf's DAA gaze proxy must approach. If GIAVA outperforms uniform processing, it sets the target for [[eval/gaze]].

**adjacent**
- [LightVLA: Towards Efficient VLA Models via Differentiable Token Pruning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6163650edbfba0a837b0722c9c49ed49040ba70d) — Jiang et al. · arXiv
  Generates dynamic queries to evaluate visual token importance based on task context, then prunes differentiably; pruning adapts as task performance improves. Mechanism comparison to surf's contrastive selector: LightVLA evaluates tokens via task-context queries (learned), surf evaluates via language-conditioned contrastive scoring (structural + language). Compare before finalizing [[experiments/contrastive alignment]] design.

- [CogVLA: Cognition-Aligned VLA via Instruction-Driven Routing & Sparsification](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/e30e7b2e70556078190f63ac50f613a2f1efd6b3) — Li et al. · arXiv
  3-stage architecture: encode → instruction-driven route → sparsify. Language instruction explicitly controls which tokens are processed. Direct instantiation of [[claims/language helps]] via routing rather than contrastive scoring. The design question: does instruction routing (faster, explicit) match or beat contrastive scoring (richer, learned) for task-relevant spatial selection?

- [π0.5: a Vision-Language-Action Model with Open-World Generalization](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1e2a82ef5e0325a26ca0344c4f8c256c52fae7ec) — Physical Intelligence et al. · arXiv
  π0 successor: co-trains on heterogeneous tasks for open-world generalization; addresses the lab-to-real gap. Relevant as the current-SOTA architectural reference surf's selector module must be composable with. Before assuming surf can attach its selection module to a large base VLA, check whether π0.5's co-training strategy creates constraints on where token selection can be inserted.

**horizon**
- [AVA-VLA: Improving Vision-Language-Action Models with Active Visual Attention](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/bf94018d5eff3065895cb856eef16181bbe4f4e9) — Xiao et al. · arXiv
  Reformulates VLA as POMDP (not MDP): history-aware attention over past observations rather than independent per-timestep processing. Orthogonal efficiency axis to surf's spatial selection — this is temporal integration, not spatial allocation. If surf's iterative selection needs observation history, AVA-VLA's POMDP formulation shows one way to integrate it inside a VLA backbone.

- [You Only Index Once: Cross-Layer Sparse Attention with Shared Routing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06467v1) — Sun et al. · arXiv 2026
  Shares a single top-k routing decision across multiple transformer layers instead of re-routing at each layer, improving long-context LLM inference efficiency. Horizon: if VLA backbones adopt cross-layer sparse attention (as they process multi-view observations + history), shared routing could reduce the cost of extracting surf's contrastive alignment signal from specific intermediate layers identified by Palacios et al. (6/4).
