## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [The Model Knows Which Tokens Matter: Automatic Token Selection via Noise Gating](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/cad7509eb8a3cb2b074c33e7f57c242853e2b149) — Landi He et al. · arXiv
  Reformulates visual token pruning as capacity-constrained communication: a Scorer+Denoiser trained on a frozen VLM using only standard next-token prediction loss determines which tokens "need" to pass. No explicit relevance labels or task-specific supervision — directly addresses the selector circular-dependency in [[questions]]. If noise gating alone achieves competitive selection, the baseline threat to [[claims/language helps]] is: what does language conditioning add beyond what next-token prediction already captures?

- [PosPrune: Visual Token Pruning with Positional Bias Correction for Efficient Large Vision-Language Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/608a7b556b1a5dad9371eae6afea8f8675028783) — Ziyang Wang et al. · AAAI 2026
  Two-stage pruning with Asymmetric Region-aware Pruning (ARP) that explicitly corrects for positional (center) bias in token selection. Must read before locking [[experiments/e001]] spec: an AAAI paper built a dedicated correction stage for center bias, which means the e001 center-bias baseline must match this level of rigor, not just a naive center crop.

- [Focus-then-Context: Subject-Centric Progressive Visual Token Reduction for Vision-Language Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/d6b178cfef9038157beb9450f9ac8f6358197236) — Yulin Zhao et al. · arXiv
  Progressive subject-centric reduction: first focus on the instruction-aligned visual subject, then preserve surrounding context — one-shot pruning discards context needed for grounding. Tests the core assumption in [[claims/iterative selection]] (multiple passes outperform one-shot top-k at equal compute) and adds a concrete finding: subject-only selection without context degrades spatial grounding, which constrains how surf's structural prior should allocate its budget.

**adjacent**
- [MoLe-VLA: Dynamic Layer-skipping Vision Language Action Model via Mixture-of-Layers for Efficient Robot Manipulation](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/5b2142f267139a69eb1d9c598ae3291fead7e4e7) — Rongyu Zhang et al. · AAAI 2026
  Layer-level adaptive sparsification for VLA via a Spatial-Temporal Aware Router conditioned on robot proprioception; 59 citations, AAAI. Competing efficiency axis: token pruning (surf) vs. layer skipping (MoLe). The efficiency/RL module in [[roadmap]] needs to distinguish token-budget allocation from this — and a natural question is whether the two are orthogonal (composable) or competing.

- [Theory of Space: Can Foundation Models Construct Spatial Beliefs through Active Exploration?](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/349a6bbf00dcf13be0904a8253afd13dc36e8895) — Pingyue Zhang et al. · arXiv
  Proposes "Theory of Space" as a benchmark for whether foundation models can actively explore to build spatial beliefs under partial observability. Directly feeds the active-perception thread Dr. Lee framed (2026-05-24 meeting) and Tianqin's project: the benchmark makes "spatial belief construction" measurable, which is exactly what surf's structural prior could be evaluated against.

- [π0.5: a Vision-Language-Action Model with Open-World Generalization](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1e2a82ef5e0325a26ca0344c4f8c256c52fae7ec) — Physical Intelligence et al. · arXiv
  Physical Intelligence's open-world VLA; 902 citations. Sets the current SOTA bar via internet-scale pretraining + robot fine-tuning — processes all visual tokens and generalizes broadly. Surf's efficiency claims must be evaluated against a π0.5-style dense baseline: if full-token processing generalizes well, the argument for token pruning needs to specify where and why pruning helps rather than hurts generalization.

**horizon**
- [Conformal Thinking: Risk Control for Reasoning on a Compute Budget](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/97d8b24e6bab5b16732cda0cce7b75c66f474cf1) — Xi Wang et al. · arXiv
  Applies conformal prediction to adaptive token budgets for reasoning LLMs: bounds the error rate while minimizing compute spend. The stopping-criterion framing maps directly onto surf's efficiency/RL module question — when to stop requesting patches — and offers a non-RL, coverage-guaranteed formulation that avoids a learned value function. Worth checking whether the conformal guarantee holds when the distribution shifts (new tasks, new environments).

- [Machine Mental Imagery: Empower Multimodal Reasoning with Latent Visual Tokens](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/b36c3b79d678805b74025497bf3abc6cbe0ee1eb) — Zeyuan Yang et al. · arXiv
  VLMs generate latent visual tokens during decoding to "imagine" spatial scenarios, improving reasoning without full image generation. Counterpoint to surf's direction: one axis compresses visual input (token pruning), another expands it during reasoning (visual imagination). If tasks requiring spatial reasoning benefit from generated visual tokens at decode time, adaptive compute may need to be bidirectional — budget-in and budget-out.

## questions
- AutoSelect trains without explicit relevance labels — does the noise-gating signal (capacity-constrained communication residual) correlate with task relevance for action tasks, or only with token informativeness to the LM head? If the latter, noise gating and surf's structural prior are measuring different things.
- PosPrune's ARP corrects "positional bias" with asymmetric region weights — is this the same center bias that surf's e001 baseline should control for, or a finer-grained spatial prior (e.g., object-boundary proximity, not just center proximity)?
- Focus-then-Context finds the instruction-aligned "subject" for progressive reduction — how is the subject localized in manipulation scenes where the relevant region is typically the workspace or gripper-object contact zone, not a salient foreground object?
- Theory of Space benchmarks spatial belief construction — does it condition exploration on language queries (Tianqin's framing) or test open-ended spatial mapping? The conditioning vs. open-ended distinction matters for whether the benchmark applies to surf's instruction-conditioned selection.
