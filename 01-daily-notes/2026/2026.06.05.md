### digest (auto)

**core**
- [AffordanceVLA: A Vision-Language-Action Model Empowering Action Generation through Affordance-Aware Understanding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06155v1) — Yu et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Addresses the structural mismatch between VLM semantic spaces and embodied control policies by encoding affordance-aware perception explicitly before action generation. Direct answer to [[questions]] "Are affordance/contact regions recoverable from structural cues alone?" — AffordanceVLA says no; affordance grounding requires dedicated perception-action bridging, not just structural patch selection. Frames the challenge surf's prior module faces: selecting structurally-salient patches may not recover action-relevant affordance regions without task conditioning.

- [GuidedVLA: Specifying Task-Relevant Factors via Plug-and-Play Action Attention Specialization](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/e53d86ef632de55eb728b048871f264442331248) — Jia et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Without explicit guidance, VLAs overfit to visual shortcuts; GuidedVLA makes task-relevant visual factors explicit via plug-and-play attention specialization modules inserted into the action decoding path. Mechanism-level comparison to surf's contrastive selector: both try to make task-relevant visual features explicit, but GuidedVLA operates at action-decoding time while surf's selector operates at observation encoding time. Compare before finalizing [[experiments/contrastive alignment]] design.

- [GeoWeaver: Grounding Visual Tokens with Geometric Evidence before Scene Reasoning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6097968f42912a0e1c84cd12311e59546f7c10d5) — Miao et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Pre-reasoning geometric grounding of visual tokens as a prerequisite for spatio-temporal intelligence; geometry must be preserved before semantic processing, not injected at reasoning time. Architectural support for surf's structural prior at the representation level: the prior module is not an efficiency trick but a representation prerequisite. Geometry-first framing is a cleaner framing for related work than "efficient inference."

**adjacent**
- [TempoVLA: Learning Speed-Controllable Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06491v1) — Jing et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Phase-aware VLA that adapts execution speed based on detected risk phase: fast transit, slow contact. A parallel adaptive compute axis to surf's spatial token selection — temporal speed vs. spatial coverage. Design observation: contact-phase detection and spatial token allocation may be composable (fewer tokens during transit, higher spatial coverage during contact). Note for [[roadmap]] efficiency/RL module design.

- [Colosseum V2: Benchmarking Generalization for Vision Language Action Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/ff60e668c280b09db7875904a8f2e768261f899c) — Morgan et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Comprehensive VLA generalization benchmark specifically measuring distribution shift robustness — finds high-level VLM capabilities do not reliably transfer to task performance under shifts. Eval design relevance: if surf's token pruning preserves task-relevant structural features, performance under Colosseum V2's distribution shifts is a direct probe of whether the selected tokens carry generalizable signal. Add alongside RoboSemanticBench.

**horizon**
- [Adaptive state-action abstractions via rate-distortion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06123v1) — Rosas · arXiv Jun 2026
  Formalizes the coarsen-then-refine decision in RL using rate-distortion theory: operate at coarse abstraction until further practice at that resolution stops paying off, then refine. Strongest theoretical framing yet for surf's adaptive visual budget: the "when to request more visual compute" decision is exactly an adaptive abstraction-granularity decision. R-D objective formalizes what BRPO/ElegantVLA/VLA-ATTC/AC²-VLA describe mechanistically. Compare before committing to stopping-criterion design.

- [Pretraining Recurrent Networks without Recurrence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06479v1) — Kumar, Isola · arXiv Jun 2026
  Supervised Memory Training (SMT) — trains RNNs without BPTT by using local objectives, enabling parallelism and avoiding gradient pathologies. By Phillip Isola (tracked). Horizon relevance: if surf's efficiency/RL module processes sequential observations via a recurrent policy, BPTT-free training may matter for training stability and scalability. Weak connection; note and move on.
