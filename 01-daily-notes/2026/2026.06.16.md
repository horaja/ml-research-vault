## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [GeoWeaver: Grounding Visual Tokens with Geometric Evidence before Scene Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22558) — Miao, Huang, Gu, Li, Zhang, Yang · arXiv
  Argues that different visual tokens require different amounts of geometric evidence rather than one shared geometric signal applied uniformly across all tokens. Directly relevant to [[claims/structure helps]]: surf's line prior is currently applied as a single uniform structural signal across patches. If per-token geometric weighting is the right granularity, a uniform line-drawing prior may be miscalibrated for some patches and wasted on others — worth reading to see whether their per-token weighting mechanism generalizes from scene reasoning to patch selection.

- [When Attention Collapses: Stage-Aware Visual Token Pruning from Structure to Semantics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03569) — Wang, Zhang, Han, Zhang · arXiv
  Shows attention-score-based pruning collapses onto semantically similar regions, losing structural diversity; proposes a two-stage structure-then-semantics pruning pipeline (STS). Relevant to both [[claims/structure helps]] (structure-first pruning as an explicit baseline category, distinct from attention-importance pruning) and [[claims/iterative selection]] (a two-stage pipeline is architecturally close to coarse-to-fine, though staged on structure-vs-semantics rather than uncertainty-vs-refinement).

- [DCP-Prune: Ultra-Low Token Pruning with Distribution Consistency Preservation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16633) — Xue, Wang, Li, Cheng, Sun · arXiv
  Finds that token-pruning accuracy degradation correlates with feature-distribution shift between retained and full tokens, and targets the ultra-low-budget regime specifically, where most pruning methods become unstable. Relevant to [[claims/efficiency without collapse]]: distribution-consistency is a second candidate operational metric for "collapse" (alongside TokenCarve's attention-output mutual information from the 6/13 digest), and the ultra-low-budget regime is exactly where surf's visual-budget claims most need to hold.

**adjacent**
- [Geometric Action Model for Robot Policy Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17046) — Han, Jeon, Jung, Zurbrügg, An, Portela, Hutter, Pollefeys, Kim, Hong · arXiv
  Argues VLAs and video world-action models operate on 2D image frames or 2D-derived latents, leaving 3D geometry implicit for contact-rich manipulation, and repurposes a pretrained geometric backbone for language-conditioned manipulation. A different structural-prior instantiation than surf's (3D geometric backbone features vs. 2D line drawings) and a different role (representation backbone vs. patch-selection signal) — worth tracking as a neighboring formulation of "structure helps," not yet a direct comparison.

- [Vision-MoR: Scaling Vision Transformer via Patch-Level Mixture-of-Recursions](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/927df060e700a323113490586904b17ffcdb9a9d) — He, Yuan, Sun, Li, Liu, Ye, Sun · AAAI
  Unifies parameter sharing, spatially adaptive computation, and memory efficiency by letting a router assign different numbers of recursion steps per patch. Adjacent to surf's efficiency/RL module framing: depth-adaptive compute per patch is a different axis than token-count selection (more like MoLe-VLA's layer-skipping, noted 6/14, but at patch granularity instead of whole-sequence layer granularity).

- [Qwen-VLA: Unifying Vision-Language-Action Modeling across Tasks, Environments, and Robot Embodiments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30280) — Wang, Li, Guan, Ye, Xie et al. (40 authors) · arXiv
  Large unified VLA foundation model spanning manipulation, navigation, and trajectory generation across embodiments, built on the Qwen vision-language stack. No explicit token-selection or structural-prior mechanism reported in the abstract — included as an industry-scale signal of where VLA generalist-policy investment is concentrated, for awareness rather than direct technical overlap.

**horizon**
- [Exploratory Experience Shapes the Geometry of Predictive Representations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27929) — Shilova, Sharafeldin, Balakrishnan, Choi · arXiv
  Studies how exploratory vs. exploitative behavioral strategies in an action-perception loop shape the internal geometry of predictive representations, using a predictive-coding agent in a maze task. Horizon: this is active sensing / representation geometry from a computational-neuroscience angle, not VLA-specific, but it bears on the [[questions]] entry asking how surf's task-conditioned patch selection relates to Tianqin's question-conditioned active-perception framing — a candidate computational framework for that connection, unverified beyond the abstract.

- [Fantastic Pretraining Optimizers and Where to Find Them II: Hyperball Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16899) — Wen, Dang, Lyu, Ma, Liang · arXiv
  Sequel to the widely-cited optimizer-comparison paper; proposes constraining weight and update Frobenius norms to fixed constants, yielding 20-30% pretraining speedup for Muon over weight-decay baselines at up to 1.2B params. No direct surf connection — included for general infrastructure awareness, since pretraining efficiency work from this group has previously reshaped default optimizer choices.
