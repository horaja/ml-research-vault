## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [DIRECT: When and Where Should You Allocate Test-Time Compute in Embodied Planners?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12402v1) — Dao, Ganai, Finn, Pavone et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Argues that uniform test-time compute scaling in embodied planners (VLMs as high-level planners) yields uneven and diminishing returns; proposes selective compute allocation conditioned on "when" in the plan and "where" in the action space compute is valuable. From Chelsea Finn's group (tracked author). Directly operationalizes surf's efficiency/RL module open question from [[questions]]: "should adaptive compute be trained as a policy, a threshold rule, or a value-of-information objective?" — DIRECT's framework is the clearest external answer to date.

- [Reroute, Don't Remove: Recoverable Visual Token Routing for Vision-Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12412v1) — Yang, Lo, Liu · arXiv Jun 2026
  Shows rank-and-remove token pruning is fragile because token importance shifts across decoder depth — tokens ranked low at shallow layers become relevant at deeper ones. Proposes routing low-ranked tokens to a recoverable pool accessible at later decoder stages rather than permanently discarding them. Directly relevant to surf's design: if line-guided patch selection hard-discards spatial tokens before the LLM backbone, it may drop tokens that become action-relevant at deeper cross-attention layers. Rerouting is an architectural alternative to hard pruning that also addresses the Cho et al. (6/10) distortion concern — and the two papers together argue for recoverable, not irreversible, spatial token reduction.

- [When Does Language Matter? Multilingual Instructions Reveal Step-wise Language Sensitivity in VLA Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11906v1) — Dong et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  First systematic multilingual VLA evaluation (LIBERO, ten languages); reveals severe degradation under non-English and, more importantly for surf, shows that language sensitivity varies step-wise within an episode — some action steps depend heavily on instruction while others are nearly instruction-agnostic. Directly relevant to [[claims/language helps]] and the CT-VAM question (6/9): if language sensitivity is step-variable, surf's language-conditioned selector should re-score patches at language-sensitive steps only, not every step — this is the empirical evidence the spec's "selector re-run frequency" section needs.

**adjacent**
- [DAM-VLA: Decoupled Asynchronous Multimodal Vision Language Action Model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12105v1) — Vanjani, Li, Suliga, Reuss · arXiv Jun 2026
  Formally decouples modality update rates in VLA: language at episode scale, vision at action-step scale, proprioception at high frequency. Synchronous VLA wastes compute oversampling language. Adjacent to surf's timing design: DAM-VLA's decoupling principle implies surf's language-conditioned selector logically runs at language-update frequency — consistent with CT-VAM (6/9) and the step-wise language sensitivity result above. Together these three papers converge on the same architectural implication: language-conditioned patch selection is an episode-start or scene-change operation, not a per-step one.

- [World Pilot: Steering Vision-Language-Action Models with World-Action Priors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12403v1) — Lin et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Augments VLA policy with a World-Action Model (WAM) that generates predicted-state priors injected into the decision chain. Adjacent mechanism comparison to surf's prior module: both inject structured top-down spatial bias into VLA inference, but World Pilot operates at world-state (future-frame prediction) while surf operates at spatial-token (patch selection) level. Relevant to [[claims/structure helps]]: World Pilot's world-state prior and surf's structural prior are complementary rather than competing — a deployed system might compose both.

- [Q-Fold: Query-Aware Focus-Context Spatio-Temporal Folding for Long Video Understanding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12125v1) — Tang et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Partitions video tokens into "focus" (high-detail, query-relevant) and "context" (compressed, query-irrelevant) based on query-token similarity, then applies differentiated compute to each partition. The focus/context two-class token treatment is structurally analogous to surf's selected/discarded design — same basic principle applied to video frames rather than spatial patches. Relevant as a design reference: Q-Fold's differentiated-compression approach (retain focus at full resolution, compress context rather than discard) is a middle ground between hard pruning and full-context processing.

**horizon**
- [BEACON: Bayesian Optimal Stopping for Efficient LLM Sampling](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/047808f3b76cb1e277bf9ff712668a8224e3b9ba) — Bruns-Smith et al. · arXiv Oct 2025
  Sequential Bayesian decision framework for when to stop generating additional LLM samples, maintaining a posterior over reward distributions and stopping when the posterior's expected gain falls below a threshold. Horizon: surf's iterative refinement module needs a stopping criterion — "when should the selector refine rather than stop?" is open in [[questions]]. BEACON's Bayesian posterior over "has quality converged?" is a principled framework for this; the key translation question is whether surf's per-step patch-selection quality can be estimated well enough to serve as the sufficient statistic.

- [IBNorm: Information-Bottleneck Inspired Normalization for Representation Learning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/b09305aa165df944d8085719cd5e2c0aeaac8e7b) — arXiv Oct 2025
  Proposes IB-inspired normalization (IBNorm) that applies bounded compression to encourage embeddings to preserve predictive information while suppressing nuisance variability, in contrast to variance-only normalization (BatchNorm/LayerNorm/RMSNorm). Horizon: surf's contrastive alignment experiment produces patch embeddings scored against language instructions. Standard LayerNorm normalizes variance but not task-relevance of the learned patch representation. IBNorm would encourage the patch encoder to discard nuisance spatial detail and retain action-predictive structure — directly applicable as a normalization choice in the contrastive selector architecture.

## questions

- DIRECT compute allocation: what signal does DIRECT use to determine "when" compute is worth spending — plan step index, uncertainty estimate, or task-completion probability? If uncertainty-based, compare to BEACON's Bayesian posterior — both are estimating "is more compute worth it?" from different angles.
- Reroute recovery depth: at which decoder depth does token importance reversal most often occur? If the flip happens in early-to-mid layers, surf could use a shallow routing gate rather than full-depth routing — cheaper than full rerouting, more robust than hard pruning.
- Step-wise language sensitivity: what distinguishes language-sensitive steps from language-agnostic steps in the LIBERO tasks? Is it proximity to language-described objects (manipulation target), or task phase (approach vs. grasp vs. place)? The answer determines whether surf's re-scoring trigger should be spatial (proximity to instruction-referenced region) or temporal (task phase transition).
- DAM-VLA decoupling: does the language module in DAM-VLA share any activations with the per-step visual module, or are they fully independent computation paths? If shared, decoupling breaks the cross-modal attention that surf's contrastive selector relies on.
- Carried from 6/10: CT-VAM re-scoring frequency; ProbeAct-Jin et al. SAE cross-validation for layer selection; PosPrune ARP center bias correction; positional encoding coherence after non-contiguous patch selection (Cho et al.).
