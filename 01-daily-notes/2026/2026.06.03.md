## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [RESTORE: Improving Visual Token Reduction via Rectifying Distortions for Efficient Multimodal LLM Inference](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/520ba8851fe0453ca99d42a77e51c62aa592821a) — Cho et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Identifies two VTR failure modes: positional distortions (pruned tokens shift spatial layout of remaining tokens) and attentional distortions (removing attention-key tokens changes what remaining tokens attend to). Extends PosPrune's finding (6/1) from architectural center bias to the general class of representation distortions that any patch reduction introduces — surf's structural prior needs to select tokens that minimize downstream distortion, not just maximize a local importance score.

- [Where to Refine, When to Stop: Rethinking Redundancy via Latent Discrepancy for Efficient Visual Autoregressive Generation](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/f99a38ae6efcb4ef0b57cac42316d7b2d297f40c) — Mei et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Uses latent discrepancy (coarse vs. refined representation divergence) as a dual signal: high discrepancy → where to add detail; low discrepancy → when to stop. Domain is VAR image generation, not VLA inference, but the stopping criterion mechanism maps directly to surf's efficiency/RL module: latent discrepancy as a value-of-information signal for "is more compute worth it?" Fifth formulation in the field alongside BRPO, ElegantVLA, VLA-ATTC, AC²-VLA — compare before committing.

- [How to Instruct Your Robot: Dense Language Annotations Power Robot Policy Learning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/280d372e919ee12a1282d3943d5b397793726beb) — Kim, Wang, Acuna, Choi et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Studies language annotation density (coarse goal vs. fine-grained per-step instructions) as an axis for robot policy quality. Directly relevant to [[claims/language helps]]: if instruction granularity determines how much language can help a policy, surf's contrastive selector on coarse instructions may be operating below the informative language bandwidth. Complements FineVLA (5/31 digest) — two concurrent papers arguing granularity matters, not just presence of language.

**adjacent**
- [World Models for Robotic Manipulation: A Survey](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/b8cc6e9645268e5b2316c55274398575cb0a67e5) — Wang et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Surveys learned world models for manipulation: predictive representations, geometry forecasting, contact modeling. Timely given AHEAD (6/2 digest) introduced dynamic scene change as a new failure axis for surf's static patch selection. Maps which world model approaches are compatible with observation-time static selection (could compose with surf) vs. which require step-level selection updates (would require redesigning surf's selection granularity).

- [Exploratory Experience Shapes the Geometry of Predictive Representations](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/32fbfdc2a3af37f998167a40607656c9af6be023) — Shilova, Sharafeldin, Balakrishnan, H. Choi · arXiv May 2026
  Shows that active exploration determines which observations enter the predictive model, shaping the geometry of learned representations through the action-perception loop. Relevant to the Lee meeting (2026-05-24) active perception reframing: if the action-perception loop shapes which visual features become predictive, surf's passive observation-time patch selection may yield different features than an actively-learned counterpart. Relevant to Tianqin conversation.

- [ProgVLA: Progress-Aware Robot Manipulation Skill Learning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/ae4fbe6ef22c468a58c01d088228940a5571befb) — Kim, Choi, Baek, Renders · arXiv May 2026
  Compact VLA for manipulation under tight compute and memory budgets; uses progress-aware context resampling to attend to task-critical regions as the task advances. Demonstrates compute-constrained VLA manipulation is feasible with architectural attention control. Progress awareness (how far along is the current task) is a potential input to surf's efficiency/RL module distinct from action confidence (VLA-ATTC), reasoning depth (ElegantVLA), and budget-relative reward (BRPO).

**horizon**
- [LookWhen? Fast Video Recognition by Learning When, Where, and What to Compute](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/3d1cb0e91d19774050a964a9d58b17ab25d6ac7d) — Salamatian, Fuller, Shelhamer et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Frames video recognition efficiency as a tri-axis problem: *when* to process (temporal), *where* to look (spatial), *what* features to compute (channel/depth). The decomposition maps structurally onto surf's three-module architecture (prior: where; action: what; efficiency/RL: when/how much). Video ≠ VLA manipulation, but if the tri-axis decomposition produces better efficiency tradeoffs than each axis alone, that supports surf's three-module design as the right granularity.

- [StateVLM: A State-Aware Vision-Language Model for Robotic Affordance Reasoning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/5ae5cf2baed85aabefb76f3a7c232a59aede38ad) — Sun, Kerzel, Li, Zhao, Wermter · arXiv May 2026
  Argues affordance reasoning requires explicit robot and object state context that visual features alone cannot recover; encodes state alongside vision for manipulation. Direct challenge to [[claims/structure helps]] and [[questions]] ("Are affordance/contact regions recoverable from structural cues alone?"): StateVLM says structural priors are insufficient if state context is not encoded. Baseline design for surf needs a state-conditioned affordance oracle, not just center-bias and Canny controls.
