## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [LookWhen? Fast Video Recognition by Learning When, Where, and What to Compute](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/3d1cb0e91d19774050a964a9d58b17ab25d6ac7d) — Salamatian et al. · 2026
  Selector-extractor framework that factorizes recognition into when/where/what to compute over space-time tokens — almost the surf framing applied to video. Strong conceptual baseline for [[claims/iterative selection]] and a compute-matched reference for [[experiments/e001]] beyond the dense-encoder control.
- [VLA-ATTC: Adaptive Test-Time Compute for VLA Models with Relative Action Critic](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/8751f40ad473a5269b710b1696d53643b7fd77cb) — Li et al. · 2026
  Uncertainty-gated "cognitive clutch" that switches a VLA between reflexive execution and deliberative test-time compute. Closest existing instantiation of surf's adaptive-allocation story inside a VLA — but the budget axis is decoding depth, not spatial patches, so it sets up a clean contrast for [[concepts/visual budget]].
- [GuidedVLA: Specifying Task-Relevant Factors via Plug-and-Play Action Attention Specialization](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/e53d86ef632de55eb728b048871f264442331248) — Jia et al. · 2026
  Explicit task-relevance guidance injected into VLA action attention to avoid visual-shortcut overfitting. Directly engages [[questions]] on what makes a patch task-relevant for action; the explicit-guidance variant is a baseline-that-could-kill a learned-selector framing of [[claims/structure helps]].

**adjacent**
- [ERASE: Eliminating Redundant Visual Tokens via Adaptive Two-Stage Token Pruning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/86a0f7b63f606ffc55770acb48b9dc76b10fa9c3) — Lee et al. · 2026
  Content-adaptive pruning ratio for VLM visual tokens — adds an "adaptive ratio" variant to the [[experiments/e001]] kill-baseline family alongside Bolya/Rao-style fixed-rate baselines.
- [RL Token: Bootstrapping Online RL with Vision-Language-Action Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/951c78ccafa7eb56f8abfe356d1b79102788e6ff) — Xu et al. · 2026
  Adds a compact "RL token" readout to a pretrained VLA for sample-efficient online RL. Relevant to the efficiency/RL module in [[roadmap]]: what readout the adaptive-allocation controller should consume, and whether it should be a learned compact head rather than raw features.
- [LAGO: Language-Guided Adaptive Object-Region Focus for Zero-Shot Visual-Text Alignment](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/a0ea94bf2d90d61f1100f610340fae596ae7337b) — Hu et al. · 2026
  Language-guided adaptive region focus for fine-grained recognition. A non-manipulation testbed for [[claims/language helps]] — if their language-guided focus only beats random crops (not a language-removed learned scorer), that mirrors the zero-language ablation surf still owes.

**horizon**
- [Gated DeltaNet-2: Decoupling Erase and Write in Linear Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22791v1) — arXiv 2026-05-21
  Separates the "how much to erase" and "how much to write" gates in linear-attention memory updates. Tangential to spatial selection, but the edit-without-scrambling primitive is the right shape for a selector that incrementally refines its allocation under [[claims/iterative selection]].
- [ViTok-v2: Scaling Native Resolution Auto-Encoders to 5 Billion Parameters](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/2f9ee6d8032b8b3a5d3b0bd26270bced5dc66c7e) — Hansen-Estruch et al. · 2026
  Follow-up to yesterday's ViTok scaling note: pushes the reconstruction–generation Pareto with a 5B native-resolution tokenizer. Worth tracking for [[eval/reconstruction]] design choices alongside the [[He et al. (2021)]] MAE baseline.

## questions
- Does GuidedVLA's explicit task-relevance specification beat a learned selector at matched compute? If yes, [[claims/structure helps]] needs to defend why an implicit structural prior is preferable to an explicit, plug-and-play action-attention spec.
- LookWhen's selector-extractor factorization in video vs. surf's static-frame allocation — is the "when to compute" axis a missing dimension in [[claims/iterative selection]], or a separate budget?
- VLA-ATTC's deliberation budget is decoding depth, not patches. Is the right unification of these two adaptive axes a single value-of-information objective, or do they decouple?
- For [[claims/structure helps]]: would a Canny edge-density baseline at matched patch count explain the [[experiments/e001]] gain?
