Today's priority is clarifying surf's differentiation from ST-Merge before writing the spec. ST-Merge exploits temporal token redundancy across video frames in VLA inference; surf exploits structural spatial density within a single frame. The two regimes are largely orthogonal but may compose or compete depending on how token budgets interact across the temporal and spatial axes. The differentiation argument in the spec requires knowing what ST-Merge actually does — read it before writing the novelty section.

Second: PAGE (2607.04860) adds a second lightweight gaze estimation baseline alongside DeepGaze3.5-VL (7/6). The evaluation picture is sharpening: PAGE gives per-frame fixation targets without task conditioning; DeepGaze3.5-VL gives task-conditioned sequential scanpaths. For surf's patch selection, both are needed — PAGE for spatial co-location validation per frame, DeepGaze3.5-VL for task-conditioned sequential alignment. Together they test two distinct gaze hypotheses and prevent confounding appearance saliency with task relevance.

## questions

- ST-Merge temporal vs. spatial: ST-Merge removes tokens that are redundant across video frames in time; surf selects tokens that are structurally informative within a frame. On typical manipulation video (slow motion, static background), how much spatial overlap is there between temporally-redundant tokens and structurally-dense tokens? If ST-Merge removes mostly background tokens and surf selects mostly foreground contact-region tokens, the two selections are nearly complementary — which means composing them (ST-Merge first, then surf within the surviving tokens) is a natural experimental design.
- PAGE vs. DeepGaze3.5-VL as eval signals: PAGE predicts where a viewer fixates in a scene without knowing the task; DeepGaze3.5-VL predicts task-conditioned sequential fixation trajectories. For surf, which signal is more informative? If surf's structural prior selects patches that match PAGE but not DeepGaze3.5-VL, the prior is capturing appearance saliency, not task-conditioned attention. If it matches DeepGaze3.5-VL but not PAGE, the structural signal is encoding task relevance beyond saliency. The discrepancy between the two baselines is the interesting measurement.
- InternVLA-A1.5 Latent Foresight: What is "Latent Foresight" operationally — goal-conditioned visual prediction (predict future scene), internal planning rollout (predict action trajectory in latent space), or something else? If it's visual prediction before selection, it directly instantiates the Bridge-WA (7/4) action-induced-change selection criterion — making InternVLA-A1.5 the strongest existing test case for whether that selection criterion helps.
- Pretraining curricula × scorer fine-tuning: if pretraining curricula identify which parameters encode visual semantics vs. motor execution, can surf use the same curricula to determine where to place the patch scorer — in the semantic layers before the action layers, supporting Palacios et al.'s layer placement finding?
