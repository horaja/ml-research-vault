**core**
- [GSPA: Geo-Semantic Prior-Guided Attention for Long-Horizon Robot Manipulation](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/593fe3f7bbdcbd6169f9903fcab28e8e65316c4e) — K. Che, L. Wang, Y. Wen, H. Wang, Y. Cheng · Semantic Scholar
  The "geo-semantic" framing is surf's two-stage claim stated explicitly: geometric structure guides where attention goes; semantic content conditions what matters. If GSPA implements both a geometric stage and a language-semantic stage for VLA attention in manipulation, it subsumes surf's combined claim — and this cannot be assessed without reading the paper. Day 13. Read before writing a single line of spec novelty.

- [Improving Vision-Language-Action Model Fine-Tuning with Structured Stage and Keyframe Supervision](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26801v1) — Y. Xu, Y. Chen, K. Wang, J. Yang, P. Li · arXiv
  Replaces uniform timestep supervision with stage-structured + keyframe-focused supervision during VLA fine-tuning — same "not all steps are equal" insight that [[claims/iterative selection]] targets at inference time. If the keyframes selected for high supervision weight are the same geometrically active frames surf's line prior would select (contact, grasp, release transitions), this training-side intervention is an implicit structural prior, and surf's explicit inference-time prior adds only marginal novelty. The overlap hypothesis is testable: compare keyframe distribution with line-density maps on the same scenes.

- [TOPS: First-Principles Visual Token Pruning via Constructing Token Optimal Preservation Sets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27161v1) — T. Wang, Y. Guo, R. Huang, Z. Lu, Q. Zhang · arXiv
  Constructs a pruning criterion from first principles — Token Optimal Preservation Sets — for efficient MLLM inference. Surf's geometric prior is a structural heuristic; TOPS may offer the theory that validates or undercuts it. Key read: what is the first-principles criterion? If it's query-conditioned (requires task input), it aligns with surf's contrastive scorer. If it's training-free and geometry-free, it is the cleanest task-agnostic token-pruning baseline yet and must appear in surf's differentiation table.

**adjacent**
- [ACTIVE-o3: Empowering MLLMs with Active Perception via Pure Reinforcement Learning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/aae1df4156ae2088e9abb5905902b1da7eb7b6bf) — M. Zhu, H. Zhong, C. Zhao, Z. Du, M. Liu · Semantic Scholar
  Learns "where and how to look" from RL alone, without geometric priors or contrastive supervision. The key question for surf: do RL-learned active-perception policies converge on structurally dense regions (edge-rich, line-dense patches)? If yes, surf's geometric prior accelerates convergence to RL's learned solution but is not strictly necessary at inference time. If no, RL and structural priors target different spatial signals — and both are needed.

- [Learning What to Say to Your VLA: Mostly Harmless Vision Language Action Model Steering](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1f1017addf8465a0a30d16e99a1347d80cab34bd) — H. Jeong, G. Swamy, A. Bajcsy · Semantic Scholar
  Semantically similar instructions map to wildly different behaviors in VLAs; studies which instruction variations are "mostly harmless" vs. behavior-critical. Adjacent to [[claims/language helps]] from the input side: if the language-to-behavior mapping is unstable at the instruction level, surf's spatial-level contrastive scorer may be insufficient without first stabilizing what the language signal means to the VLA backbone. Bajcsy at CMU; this is the CMU robotics lab's current take on language conditioning.

- [MoLe-VLA: Dynamic Layer-skipping VLA via Mixture-of-Layers for Efficient Manipulation](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/5b2142f267139a69eb1d9c598ae3291fead7e4e7) — R. Zhang, M. Dong, Y. Zhang, L. Heng, X. Chi · Semantic Scholar
  Achieves VLA efficiency via dynamic layer-skipping rather than token pruning — layers are skipped adaptively based on task complexity. Directly adjacent to surf's efficiency claim: layer-level and token-level sparsity are orthogonal axes, so MoLe-VLA doesn't compete with surf's spatial selection directly, but it occupies the same compute-budget narrative. Surf's efficiency argument needs to distinguish spatial-selection gains from layer-sparsity gains to avoid claiming a result that layer-skipping already covers.

**horizon**
- [Paying More Attention to Visual Tokens in Self-Evolving Large Multimodal Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27373v1) — S. Venkatraman, R. Thawkar, O. Thawakar, R. M. Anwer, H. Cholakkal · arXiv
  Self-evolving LMMs using self-play and consistency rewards systematically underweight visual tokens relative to linguistic tokens; proposes visual-token-aware reward schemes to correct the imbalance. Horizon connection: surf's structural prior is the supply-side version of this correction — explicitly ensuring geometrically informative visual tokens are not crowded out. If the visual underweighting is systematic across training paradigms (not just self-play), the case for explicit structural priors strengthens as VLAs scale and linguistic dominance grows.

- [Rényi Attention Entropy for Patch Pruning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6e5d15491f05036d7c17dd72a75d2e0a9a2c6b54) — H. Aizawa, Y. Igaue · Semantic Scholar
  Uses Rényi entropy of attention distributions as a patch pruning criterion — patches contributing more to attention entropy are retained. An information-theoretic formalization of the same intuition behind surf's structural prior: high-entropy patches carry more information. If high-Rényi-entropy patches correlate with structurally dense patches (edge-rich, line-drawing-dense regions), Rényi entropy is an alternative single-feature baseline for surf's geometric claim and must appear in the spec's differentiation table. If they diverge, they are complementary selection criteria addressing different information regimes.
