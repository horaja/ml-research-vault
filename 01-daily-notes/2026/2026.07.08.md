## opener

Wednesday, July 8. GSPA unread — day 25. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 14. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 7+ weeks. ST-Merge read priority — day 2.

Today's digest lead: **ActionCache** (arXiv 2607.06370, posted today) introduces a training-free efficiency approach that targets the action denoising head rather than visual tokens — caching intermediate flow-matching actions across timesteps and refining only when the scene has changed enough to invalidate the cache. This creates the clearest separation yet between surf's efficiency axis and the temporal-redundancy approaches (ST-Merge, ActionCache): surf prunes spatially redundant visual patches at the observation stage; ActionCache prunes temporally redundant computation at the action generation stage. The two are potentially composable, but the key design question is what surf's structural prior says about when ActionCache's cache should be invalidated — if line-dense structural patches are the primary change signal between timesteps, surf's selection can serve as a computationally cheap invalidation criterion.

Second: **layer-wise redundancy in VLA fine-tuning** (Nguyen et al.) — severe representational redundancy means most layers encode near-identical features and only a small structural core needs updating. The implication for surf's scorer placement is concrete: if the task-relevant feature subspace is localized to a few non-redundant layers (consistent with Palacios et al.'s intermediate-layer finding), surf's contrastive scorer should be trained to operate at those layers, not globally across the full model.

The remaining critical items (spec, STS, GSPA, ST-Merge) are unchanged. Write the spec.

## questions

- ActionCache invalidation × surf structural selection: ActionCache reuses intermediate flow-matching actions until the scene has changed enough to warrant fresh inference. What triggers "changed enough"? If surf's structural patch selection (line-dense, contact-region patches) co-locates with scene-change regions between timesteps, surf's binary selection mask is a cheap invalidation signal — high structural change in selected patches → invalidate cache. If they don't co-locate, surf and ActionCache are truly orthogonal and compose cleanly with no interference.
- Object-evidence tokens × structural density: SaMer preserves object-evidence centroids; surf selects structurally dense patches. On LIBERO manipulation scenes, do object-evidence tokens and line-dense patches co-locate? If yes, the line prior is an efficient RGB-only proxy for object-evidence detection. If no, structural density captures something distinct (contact geometry, affordance edges) that object-evidence preservation misses — the divergence is the experimentally testable claim for [[claims/structure helps]].
- Layer redundancy × scorer placement: Nguyen et al. show that VLA models have severe layer-wise redundancy and a small non-redundant core. Does surf's contrastive scorer extract a signal that is maximally discriminative from the non-redundant layers? If the non-redundant layers are the same as Palacios et al.'s intermediate layers, this gives a convergent three-source answer for scorer placement.
- Action topography × structural prior: Cortinovis et al. find action-topographic organization in lateral occipitotemporal cortex, with selectivity for hands, tools, and affordance-relevant objects. Do surf's line-drawing-selected patches preferentially activate regions with high action-topographic selectivity (tools, hands, contact points)? If yes, the structural prior is implicitly selecting for the brain's action-relevant visual regions, providing an independent behavioral validation beyond gaze co-location.
