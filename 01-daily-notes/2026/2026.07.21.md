## opener

Tuesday, July 21. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 15 weeks. GSPA unread — day 38. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 27. Visual Access Boundaries unread — day 7.

Three papers in today's digest deserve attention before the spec.

**Patch Policy** (Zhou et al., arXiv 2026-07-20) argues that pretrained dense ViT features are underutilized in robot learning — current policies either compress to a single global token or train backbones from scratch. The paper uses *all* patch-level ViT features for embodied control. This is the most direct challenge yet to surf's sparse-selection framing: if dense patch features are the right unit of representation, the question is not "which patches" but "how to use all of them cheaply." Read before writing the spec. The diagnostic: does Patch Policy process all patch tokens, or does it still select a subset of dense embeddings? If all tokens, it is a competing approach; if a subset, surf's contrastive scorer could be the selection frontend for Patch Policy-style dense features.

**FM-VLA** (Li et al., arXiv 2026-07-20) uses force-based memory for VLA in contact-rich manipulation, conditioning on past images and force signals sampled specifically at contact events. This grounds what "contact-critical" means for representation: force signals concentrate at contact boundaries, which are exactly the regions surf's line-density prior selects. Proposed co-location test: compute IoU between FM-VLA's force-activated frames/regions and surf's line-density heatmap on LIBERO scenes. Divergence → surf's structural prior misses critical contact geometry; overlap → force activation and line density are proxies for the same underlying structure.

**Sequential Optimization of Multiple Stopping Times with Spatial Subset Selection** (Tayewo, MCAP 2026-07-16) provides the theory paper surf has been circling: it jointly formalizes spatial subset selection (which locations to inspect at each step) and temporal stopping (when to commit across a sequence). This maps directly onto [[claims/iterative selection]] and the probing-based SHT framework (Kumar et al., 7/18). If surf's budget policy can be expressed within this framework, stopping criteria become principled rather than heuristic.

Write the spec.

  Memory-augmented VLA that conditions on past images sampled at contact events and integrates force signals; improves performance in contact-rich tasks where visual-only memory is insufficient. Contact-event sampling is an empirical instantiation of surf's structural prior claim: the policy needs information exactly at contact boundaries. Proposed test: does force-activated region IoU with line-density heatmap on LIBERO contact scenes? - [Sequential Optimization of Multiple Stopping Times with Spatial Subset Selection](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/705b026b8fb7fe095a48e3a8ddeee13896822310) — R. Tayewo · Methodology and Computing in Applied Probability (2026-07-16)
  Formal sequential decision problem jointly optimizing temporal stopping (when to commit) and spatial subset selection (which locations to inspect). Pure theory grounding for [[claims/iterative selection]]: the spatial component is surf's "which tokens to select at each step," the temporal component is "when to stop refining." Composing this framework with the probing-based SHT (Kumar et al., 7/18) gives a principled budget policy: stop iterating when the expected gain from one more selection step falls below the per-step cost.

## questions

- Patch Policy × surf architecture: Does Patch Policy process all ViT patch tokens end-to-end, or does it still perform some spatial compression? If all tokens, surf's contrastive scorer is not a replacement but a selection frontend — the question shifts to whether selecting 40-60% of dense features preserves Patch Policy-level performance at lower compute. If Patch Policy itself selects spatially, what is its selection criterion and how does it compare to line density?
- FM-VLA × [[claims/structure helps]]: FM-VLA's contact-event sampling grounds "contact-critical frames" empirically. Does IoU between FM-VLA force-activated regions and surf line-density heatmap hold on LIBERO scenes? If it holds, surf's prior is a cheap proxy for the signal FM-VLA extracts via force sensing — a cross-modal validation of [[claims/structure helps]] without requiring force hardware.
- Tayewo × [[claims/iterative selection]]: The sequential stopping + spatial subset paper formalizes both components of surf's budget policy. Can surf's contrastive scorer be cast as the spatial selection function in Tayewo's framework, and the per-step cost as the stopping criterion from Kumar et al. (7/18)? If yes, the two papers together give a principled budget policy formulation for [[experiments/contrastive alignment]].
- Reasoning architecture × surf: If think-then-act VLA is *less* robust to visual input perturbation (a possible result from Trinh et al.), surf's selective visual input stage may introduce fragility at the reasoning step. Does removing structural patches from the visual input corrupt the reasoning stage more than the action stage? This would be an argument for structural token selection at the action-encoding layer rather than at the input.
- Scale × structural prior: Does Xiaomi-Robotics-1's 100K-hour scale make structural priors redundant on LIBERO? Or do structural priors remain useful in the low-data fine-tuning regime even when the foundation model is data-saturated? The fine-tuning efficiency angle may be surf's strongest framing against data-scale competitors.
