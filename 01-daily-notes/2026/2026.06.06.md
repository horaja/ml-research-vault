### digest (auto)

**core**
- [Information-dependent eye-hand coordination emerges from active vision](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/439f4faa6ea6f868d768d06c98a2dcdbcc8bfc44) — Zhao et al. · bioRxiv Jun 2026
  Formalizes active vision as dual stochastic MPC: eye movements continuously minimize task-relevant uncertainty to build an internal model used for hand movement planning. The closest formal analog to surf's patch selection problem — eyes (selector) minimize uncertainty for hand (action module); the coupling between visual allocation and downstream motor planning is exactly what surf's three-module architecture encodes. Relevant to [[questions]] active perception thread and Tianqin conversation.

- [ElegantVLA: Learning When to Think for Efficient Vision-Language-Action Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/922638b3eac40136d0751c4f11dbdca573f128a2) — Li et al. · arXiv May 2026
  VLA that learns to switch between "slow" reasoning (full LLM chain) and "fast" action (lightweight policy head) based on task state; eliminates fixed per-step reasoning overhead. The "temporal mode switch" formulation — one of the four adaptive compute VLA formulations the meeting today oriented surf within. Compose with surf: ElegantVLA decides *when* to reason deeply; surf's spatial selector decides *where* to look during that reasoning. Two independent efficiency axes, or coupled?

- [Event-Grounded Sparse Autoencoders for Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1b1a40c218d97bb68f8ebea2ad7f5ca52f1347c4) — Jin et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Applies sparse autoencoders to VLA hidden representations; finds that manipulation-critical moments activate spatially localized token clusters in intermediate layers. Provides the empirical grounding for the contrastive alignment experiment's architecture choice: if SAE probing identifies which intermediate layers carry action-relevant spatial features, surf's contrastive signal should be extracted from those layers. Complements Palacios et al. (6/4) — VIT localization is now verifiable at the feature level.

**adjacent**
- [ERASE: Eliminating Redundant Visual Tokens via Adaptive Two-Stage Token Pruning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/86a0f7b63f606ffc55770acb48b9dc76b10fa9c3) — Lee et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Two-stage VLM token pruning: coarse pass removes globally redundant tokens, fine-grained pass applies task-conditioned selection. Directly instantiates [[claims/iterative selection]] — the coarse structural prior + task-conditioned refinement pipeline surf proposes. Compare ERASE's two-stage design against surf's planned coarse-to-fine architecture before finalizing [[experiments/contrastive alignment]] scope.

- [EarlyTom: Early Token Compression Completes Fast Video Understanding](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/7f47dbd6606d1d6fcb52226773db12fb0d830814) — Wang et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Shows that early-layer token compression (before semantic features consolidate) matches or outperforms late-layer compression in video-LLMs, while being faster. Consistent with the "When Attention Collapses" finding (6/4) — structural priors are most reliable before semantic diffusion. Design constraint for surf: if early-stage compression is sufficient, surf's structural prior does not need to wait for deep features.

- [Feature-Optimized Vision for Adaptive 3D Scene Reconstruction](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/ac34d8d7fc4eacdd2e945291eef0e3c6026f4114) — Liang · arXiv May 2026
  Proposes an adaptive visual front-end that is "more deliberate about which visual evidence to spend compute on" for geometry-critical 3D reconstruction, using structural discriminability rather than uniform feature budgets. Cross-domain parallel to surf: geometry-critical task (manipulation) + structural visual prior + adaptive compute budget. If selective compute generalizes from reconstruction to manipulation, related-work argument is tighter than VLP/classification analogies.

**horizon**
- [Channel-wise Vector Quantization](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/13bac2ea3da158e357bb1ce247208d789d27a4a6) — Song et al. · arXiv May 2026
  New visual tokenization paradigm: replaces patch-wise spatial tokens with channel-wise tokens and "next-channel prediction." If the field moves from patch tokens to channel tokens, surf's patch-selection structural prior needs rethinking. Monitor as a tokenization paradigm signal — not actionable yet, but worth noting when the next representation change arrives.

- [Beyond Surrogate Gradients: Fully Differentiable Token Pruning for Vision-Language Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/5865585d29f0c9c83b717729ec3f82edc705bed4) — He et al. · arXiv May 2026
  DiffPrune — reformulates discrete token pruning as continuous control of token information via an "Information Throttler"; avoids Gumbel-Softmax approximation. Resolves the carried question from 6/3 ("DiffPrune continuous suppression convergence vs. Gumbel-Softmax"): the answer is variance-preserving noise + continuous suppression instead of straight-through estimation. Design option for surf's selector if differentiability through the selection operation is needed for end-to-end training.
