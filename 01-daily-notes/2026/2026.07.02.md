# 2026-07-02

## opener

Thursday, July 2. GSPA unread — day 19. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 8. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 7+ weeks.

Today's lead: SpiralFovea (Mahajan & Saqlain, arXiv 2607.00780) introduces input-adaptive foveated tokenization as a "third lever" for resource-adaptive inference — alongside early exit and MoE routing. Where prior methods change what the model does, SpiralFovea changes what hits the backbone: token identity, location, scale, and count are all functions of local visual entropy, computed parameter-free before any LLM layer. This occupies surf's exact design space. The differentiation question is sharp: SpiralFovea selects tokens by content-adaptive entropy (bottom-up); surf's geometric prior selects by structural density (line/shape, task-directed). On textureless flat surfaces with geometric structure — the canonical surf use case — they should diverge. That divergence is the claim. SpiralFovea belongs in the spec differentiation table alongside ST-Merge, DepthCache, and STS.

Second: Task-Relevant Representation Decoupling for VRL (Wang et al., arXiv 2607.00796) applies contrastive disentanglement to isolate task-relevant from task-irrelevant visual representations for RL generalization. This is the formulation surf's contrastive scorer needs, in a different control domain. If their contrastive objective cleanly isolates which visual features predict task success, the design principle transfers to surf's patch-level alignment experiment. The VRL vs. VLA gap matters — but the representation-disentanglement framing is shared.

- [SpiralFovea: Input-Adaptive Foveated Tokenization as a Third Lever of Resource-Adaptive Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00780v1) — K. Mahajan, M. Saqlain · arXiv
  Parameter-free input-stage tokenizer: token identity, location, scale, and count are all functions of local visual entropy; selection completes before the backbone processes any input. Directly competes with surf's geometric prior at the tokenization stage — both decide which tokens enter the LLM, both operate without learned parameters over the visual content. Differentiation: entropy-based foveation targets content salience; surf's structural prior targets geometric density (line/shape) as a proxy for task-relevant affordance cues. Key test: on manipulation-relevant scenes, do they select the same or different patches? Must add to spec differentiation table.

- [Task-Relevant Representation Decoupling for Visual Reinforcement Learning Generalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00796v1) — J. Wang, Y. Lin, X. Hu et al. · arXiv
  Decouples task-relevant from task-irrelevant visual observations via contrastive loss to improve VRL generalization across environments. Methodological precedent for surf's contrastive scorer: if contrastive disentanglement of task-relevant visual features works in VRL, the same principle anchors [[experiments/contrastive alignment]]. Check whether their task-success prediction objective (features that predict reward across environment variations) maps onto surf's target (features that are spatially targeted by the instruction). Domain differs but representation-disentanglement formulation is shared.

Caveat: P2R operates on high-resolution VQA, not VLA manipulation; the domain gap means transfer is architectural, not empirical.

## questions

- SpiralFovea entropy vs. structural density: on manipulation-relevant scenes, do high-entropy regions co-locate with high-line-density regions? If yes, SpiralFovea is a simpler baseline that captures the same signal. If no, structural geometry encodes affordance cues that entropy misses — this is surf's differentiating claim. Test: compare SpiralFovea token masks against line-drawing-derived patch scores on LIBERO or BridgeV2 images.
- Input-stage foveation × attention logit collapse: SpiralFovea and surf's prior both reduce tokens before the backbone; tokens never encoded means their key vectors never participate in softmax, so the ERA attention logit collapse should not occur. Does this mean input-stage pruning methods (surf + SpiralFovea) are architecturally safer than scorer-stage pruning (STS stage 2, ERA targets)? Verifying this may change where surf places its token reduction step.
- VRL representation decoupling → VLA patch selection: Wang et al.'s contrastive objective identifies task-relevant features by what predicts success across environment variations. Surf's contrastive scorer should identify task-relevant patches by what patches the instruction targets spatially. Are these the same disentanglement problem? If the VRL objective (invariance to env-irrelevant variation) maps to surf's objective (spatial alignment with instruction-referenced regions), the training setup transfers.
- All-TNNs + surf prior: if topographic organization emerges from end-to-end training with a topographic loss, can a topographic ViT fine-tuned on robotic manipulation data produce a task-relevant structural prior without the Jiang et al. digital-twin pipeline? This would be the cleanest operationalization of [[claims/v4 relevance]] without biological data dependency.
