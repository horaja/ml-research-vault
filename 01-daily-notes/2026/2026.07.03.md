## opener

Friday, July 3. GSPA unread — day 20. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 9. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 7+ weeks.

Today's named concept: EADP (Wang et al., arXiv 2607.02484) identifies two failure modes in cross-modal token pruning that are directly surf-relevant. "Textual noise dispersion" — when the language signal is diffuse or ambiguous, cross-modal attention scores spread across irrelevant image regions, causing bad patch selection. "Feature fragmentation" — point-wise token scoring breaks up coherent spatial features. Surf's geometric prior is a direct defense against textual noise dispersion: by restricting the scoring domain to structurally dense patches before cross-modal scoring runs, it prevents the language signal from dispersing across featureless regions. EADP's entropy-aware structured compression is an alternative defense. Read before finalizing the contrastive alignment scorer architecture.

Second: PosA-VLA (Li et al.) independently arrives at surf's core motivation via a different path. They attribute redundant and unstable manipulation trajectories to the "spatially uniform perception field" of existing VLAs — identical to surf's motivating observation — and fix it with pose-conditioned anchor attention (anchoring to end-effector / skeleton regions). Surf's contrastive scorer conditions on language; PosA-VLA conditions on pose. Both are non-uniform spatial attention over the same input. PosA-VLA is a new ablation requirement: language-conditioned selection must beat pose-conditioned selection on instruction-driven tasks, and vice versa in pose-critical but instruction-ambiguous tasks.

**core**
- [Combating Textual Noise and Redundancy: Entropy-Aware Dense Visual Token Pruning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02484v1) — X. Wang, X. Yang, W. Shen · arXiv
  Identifies "textual noise dispersion" (cross-modal scoring spreads over irrelevant patches when instructions are noisy) and "feature fragmentation" (point-wise selection breaks coherent spatial structure) as the two root failures of VLM token pruning. Surf's geometric pre-filter is a defense against dispersion; EADP's entropy-aware structured compression is an alternative defense. Read before writing the contrastive alignment scorer to understand whether the two fixes compose or substitute.

- [PosA-VLA: Enhancing Action Generation via Pose-Conditioned Anchor Attention](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/d77a24298f448ff9f6aeabec20137aebeb4534e9) — Z. Li, X. Wang, H. Zhang · arXiv
  Attributes redundant/unstable VLA manipulation trajectories to spatially uniform perception; fixes with pose-conditioned (end-effector/skeleton) anchor attention over task-relevant regions. Independent validation of surf's core motivation from trajectory quality, not compute efficiency. Establishes a new baseline: pose-conditioning vs. instruction-conditioning. Surf must demonstrate that language-conditioned structural attention adds value over pose-conditioned attention on tasks where the instruction is the only disambiguating signal.

- [When Token Compression Breaks: Structural Pruning vs. Token Reduction for Robust ViT Segmentation under High Compression](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02237v1) — T.-P. Nguyen, N.-M. Cheung · arXiv
  Head-to-head empirical comparison of structural pruning (remove model components) vs. token reduction (fewer tokens, intact model) in ViTs under aggressive compression ratios and corrupted inputs. Token reduction degrades sharply at high compression; structural pruning is more robust to both. Caveat: segmentation ≠ VLA manipulation, but the compression breakpoint finding informs surf's design choice between scorer-stage token reduction and structural model compression.

**adjacent**
- [LIME: Learning Intent-aware Camera Motion from Egocentric Video](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02417v1) — B. Sun, J. Li, Y.-H. Yang et al. · arXiv
  Frames language-conditioned camera motion (move to reveal instruction-relevant regions before acting) as a first-class robot action; learns the camera policy from egocentric video. Surf addresses "where to look" at the patch-selection level; LIME addresses it at the view-selection level. Relevant question: do LIME's camera moves bring into frame the same structurally dense regions surf's scorer would select from a fixed view? If yes, they are coarse-to-fine complements. If no, they target different spatial resolution problems and neither substitutes for the other.

- [Guided Action Flow: Q-Guided Inference for Flow-Matching Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02092v1) — L. Yang, Z. Jiang, C. Sheng et al. · arXiv
  Test-time critic-guided sampling for flow-matching VLA (SmolVLA) without retraining; critic trained on success/failure rollouts conditioned on task description. Adjacent to [[claims/iterative selection]]: if iterative critic-guided flow steps improve actions, the analogous argument for surf is that iterative patch refinement (select → observe outcome → reselect) beats one-shot top-k. The critic conditioning on task description mirrors surf's language-conditioned scorer conditioning on instruction.

## questions

- EADP textual noise × geometric pre-filter: does restricting cross-modal scoring to high-line-density patches prevent textual noise dispersion by design? If the scoring domain contains only structurally informative patches, a noisy language signal has fewer irrelevant regions to disperse into. This is a testable claim about surf's scorer design — does structural pre-filtering reduce scoring variance on ambiguous instructions relative to full-image scoring?
- PosA-VLA pose-conditioning vs. surf language-conditioning: on manipulation scenes, which spatial regions do pose-conditioned anchor attention and instruction-conditioned contrastive attention each select? If both select contact zones and tool-engagement regions, they are solving the same problem with different priors. If pose-conditioning selects end-effector-adjacent regions and language-conditioning selects object-class-relevant regions, the two priors are complementary and surf should propose combining them as an ablation.
- Token reduction breakpoint in VLA: at what compression ratio does surf's token reduction degrade in VLA manipulation (analogous to the segmentation breakpoints in "When Token Compression Breaks")? If surf's typical budget (e.g., top-25% of patches) is above the breakpoint, token reduction is safe. If the surf paper claims aggressive reduction (top-10%), the breakpoint finding may threaten [[claims/efficiency without collapse]].
- LIME camera motion × surf patch selection composability: if LIME's camera policy and surf's contrastive scorer both respond to the same instruction, what prevents them from selecting redundant information (zooming the camera into the same region surf would anyway select tokens from)? Is there a coarse-to-fine composition (LIME provides coarse view, surf provides fine patch selection within that view) that adds more than either alone?
- Guided Action Flow × iterative selection: Guided Action Flow uses a learned critic to iteratively refine the *action* at inference time; surf proposes iterative refinement of *patch selection*. Are these dual problems (action refinement vs. observation refinement) or can the same critic architecture serve both? The success/failure conditioning in Guided Action Flow is the closest existing design to surf's hypothetical selection-value critic.
