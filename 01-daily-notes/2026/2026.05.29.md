### digest (auto)

**core**
- [Look, Focus, Act: Efficient and Robust Robot Learning via Human Gaze and Foveated Vision Transformers](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/8f7985729b7c0f0f365839dcfcf0474d5a9f6c01) — Chuang, Lee et al. · arXiv
  Implements foveated ViT guided by human gaze data for robot control: gaze-directed high-resolution processing of task-relevant regions, peripheral compression elsewhere. Companion paper to Gaze on the Prize (same lab, same first authors); reads as the applied counterpart. Directly defines what surf's gaze-prior baseline should look like — if foveated ViT with human gaze data is the implemented system, surf's structural-prior alternative must be compared against it, not just against random or center-bias selection.

- [DepthCache: Depth-Guided Training-Free Visual Token Merging for VLA Model Inference](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/349a9a94f5d47624a65a84e9125fd430f18f6fea) — Li et al.
  Uses depth maps as a structural/geometric cue to merge redundant visual tokens in VLA inference without any training. Depth is a mid-level structural prior like surf's line drawings — both capture 3D geometry without appearance. Adds a baseline to the [[experiments/e001]] spec: if depth-guided training-free merging is competitive with surf's learned line prior, the argument for the line prior narrows to cases where depth estimation fails (textureless surfaces, monocular ambiguity) or where line structure captures affordance-relevant geometry that depth does not.

- [Active-O3: Empowering Multimodal Large Language Models with Active Perception via GRPO](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/7e40accfc7ae38cea9daa21ff958cedaa7140830) — Zhu et al. · arXiv
  Trains MLLMs to actively decide where and how to look using GRPO reinforcement learning, without explicit gaze supervision; the model learns to crop and zoom task-relevant regions from reward signal alone. Directly relevant to [[claims/language helps]] and the efficiency/RL module design: GRPO-trained active perception without gaze labels is a direct competitor to surf's language-conditioned spatial selection. If RL-trained "where to look" beats instruction-conditioned patch scoring, the language-conditioning argument needs to specify what instruction specificity adds over learned reward-signal attention.

**adjacent**
- [TTF-VLA: Temporal Token Fusion via Pixel-Attention Integration for Vision-Language-Action Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/02462f0f53d1b48867e35eae8901d75996e5a662) — Liu et al. · AAAI
  Fuses temporal visual tokens across timesteps in VLA models using a pixel-attention mechanism; addresses the non-Markovian gap from the other direction (token aggregation rather than history compression). HoliTom (5/28) merged spatial+temporal tokens holistically; TTF-VLA fuses them via pixel attention in VLA specifically. Together they narrow what surf needs to claim: per-frame structural prior selection is meaningful only if temporal redundancy is NOT the dominant axis — this paper makes that a testable assumption.

- [SmolVLA: A Vision-Language-Action Model for Affordable and Efficient Robotics](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6ab4d113676d00e74b55e918fee4c7affaa8652f) — Shukor et al. · arXiv
  Compact VLA built for compute-constrained deployment; strong performance with a substantially smaller model footprint. Sets a lower-bound baseline: if a compact VLA already achieves competitive task performance, surf's token-pruning efficiency argument needs to apply on top of, not instead of, model compression — the two axes are not substitutes.

- [Beyond Surrogate Gradients: Fully Differentiable Token Pruning for Vision-Language Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/5865585d29f0c9c83b717729ec3f82edc705bed4)
  Replaces Gumbel-Softmax approximation in token pruning with a fully differentiable selector; enables end-to-end gradient flow through the selection decision. Relevant to how surf would train its selector module: if fully differentiable pruning is now tractable, the circular-dependency concern in [[questions]] (selector inputs vs. selected-patch downstream features) becomes less of a blocker — the whole system can be trained jointly.

**horizon**

  Comprehensive survey of strategies for bounding inference compute in LLMs: budget forcing, early exit, speculative decoding, adaptive chain-of-thought length. The stopping-criterion taxonomy here maps onto surf's efficiency/RL module design — "when to request more patches" is an instance of the general adaptive test-time compute problem. - [A Scaling Law for Token Efficiency in LLM Fine-Tuning Under Fixed Compute Budgets](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/01f159e5d11620a4fa57e3fafd5019df2babbc97) · arXiv
  Derives a scaling law for fine-tuning data composition under fixed compute that accounts for token efficiency, not just data volume. Peripheral but relevant: if token efficiency has a predictable relationship to downstream performance under a fixed compute budget, surf's visual budget framework may have a computable theoretical floor — the efficiency gain from token pruning is not arbitrary but follows a curve.

## questions
- Look, Focus, Act uses human gaze data for robot training — is the gaze signal used as a spatial prior at inference time (like surf's structural prior), as attention supervision during training, or as a reward signal? The mechanism determines whether it is a baseline threat or a data-collection strategy.
- DepthCache uses depth maps as a structural prior without training — what happens when monocular depth estimation fails (textureless workspaces, reflective surfaces)? Surf's line prior may be more robust in the failure cases that matter most for manipulation.
- Active-O3 learns where-to-look via GRPO without gaze supervision — does the learned attention pattern correlate with human gaze, or with task-relevant action regions? If RL reward signal and human gaze diverge (which they can in manipulation), Active-O3 and gaze-prior methods are solving different subproblems.
