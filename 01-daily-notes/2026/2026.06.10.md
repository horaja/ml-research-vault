## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [ReconVLA: Reconstructive Vision-Language-Action Model as Effective Robot Perceiver](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/c87ea2afcd9212e5128e01daf17c733d285ac345) — Song et al. · AAAI 2025
  Empirically demonstrates that VLA visual attention is consistently dispersed across the scene rather than concentrating on the task-relevant target object; proposes a reconstruction auxiliary objective to anchor attention on the correct region. The peer-reviewed, high-citation confirmation that current VLAs have dispersed attention is the empirical motivation surf needs for its structural-prior claim — use as the opening evidence in [[experiments/contrastive alignment]] spec: the problem is real, established at AAAI, and surf's structural prior is a specific mechanism to fix it.

- [Improving Visual Token Reduction via Rectifying Distortions for Efficient Multimodal LLM Inference](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/520ba8851fe0453ca99d42a77e51c62aa592821a) — Cho et al. · Jun 2026
  Identifies that existing visual token reduction methods break positional and attentional consistency between the full and reduced token sequences, introducing representational distortions downstream. Directly relevant to surf's design: line-guided patch selection produces a non-contiguous spatial subset, and if positional encoding coherence is lost, the selected patches may hurt task performance even when the structurally correct ones are chosen. Cho et al.'s rectification strategy should be evaluated as a post-selection consistency step.

- [LIBERO-Occ: Evaluating and Improving VLA Models under Scene-Induced Occlusion via Viewpoint Imagination](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10862v1) — Li et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Extends LIBERO with occlusion conditions under which task-relevant objects are partially or fully obscured; shows that state-of-the-art VLAs degrade substantially. Directly useful as an evaluation scaffold for surf: scene occlusion creates a natural ground-truth test for whether the selected patches contain the task-relevant region, measuring selection quality independent of overall accuracy. If surf's structural prior recovers the target object under occlusion where uniform processing fails, that isolates the selection contribution.

**adjacent**
- [ElegantVLA: Learning When to Think for Efficient Vision-Language-Action Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/922638b3eac40136d0751c4f11dbdca573f128a2) — Li et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Learns a per-step temporal schedule for engaging the full VLM reasoning path vs. a lightweight visuomotor path — "when to think" at each action step. First of the four adaptive-compute formulations raised at the 6/6 meeting (ElegantVLA / VLA-ATTC / BRPO / AC²-VLA) to appear in the digest. Directly relevant to surf's efficiency/RL module: temporal compute allocation (ElegantVLA) and spatial token allocation (surf) are orthogonal axes; a deployed system needs to compose them. If ElegantVLA's low-compute steps also need fewer spatial tokens, the two schedules may be jointly learnable.

- [PosA-VLA: Enhancing Action Generation via Pose-Conditioned Anchor Attention](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/d77a24298f448ff9f6aeabec20137aebeb4534e9) — Li et al. · arXiv Dec 2025
  Attributes redundant VLA actions to spatially uniform attention; proposes anchor tokens derived from predicted object pose that bias attention toward the task-relevant target region. Mechanism comparison to surf's structural prior: both break spatial uniformity, but PosA-VLA requires explicit object pose prediction as the anchor signal while surf uses line-drawing geometry. The failure mode comparison matters for [[claims/structure helps]]: if object pose is uncertain or occluded (as in LIBERO-Occ), pose-anchor fails while structural prior may still recover edges/contours.

- [TacForeSight: Force-Guided Tactile World Model for Contact-Rich Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11184v1) — Zang et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Models asymmetric spatiotemporal roles of global force sensing and local tactile feedback in contact-rich manipulation to anticipate contact transitions. Relevant to surf's persistent open question: are affordance/contact patches recoverable from structural visual cues alone? TacForeSight's contact-region localization identifies the ground-truth action-relevant patches that surf claims a structural prior recovers. If contact regions in manipulation correspond to object edges and contours (where line priors activate), the structural-contact correspondence supports [[claims/structure helps]]; if they diverge, it quantifies the structural prior's coverage gap.

**horizon**
- [When to Align, When to Predict: A Phase Diagram for Multimodal Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11190v1) — Kamai et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Derives systematic conditions for when cross-modal alignment (CA) objectives succeed vs. when cross-modal prediction (CP) objectives are required, framing this as a phase diagram over data geometry. Horizon: surf's contrastive alignment is a CA objective; the phase diagram would predict whether CA is the right objective for patch-language similarity learning or whether a prediction-based loss (predict action from selected patches) is more appropriate. Theory is for general multimodal representations, not VLA-specific — apply cautiously and verify assumptions hold in the manipulation setting before referencing.

## questions

- ReconVLA attention measure: how is "dispersed attention" operationalized — attention entropy over spatial positions, max-weight centroid offset, or coverage spread? If entropy-based, compare to Xu et al. (6/9) entropy-guided KV budgeting for LLMs — both may be measuring the same distributional collapse from different angles.
- LIBERO-Occ recovery mechanism: does the viewpoint imagination recovery operate at patch level or full-frame? If patch-level, it could augment surf's occlusion-robustness evaluation by generating partially-observed training variants.
- Rectifying distortions — positional encoding: does non-contiguous patch selection (line-guided) systematically disrupt the absolute positional encodings that the downstream VLM uses for spatial reasoning? If yes, Cho et al.'s rectification or a learned positional re-indexing is a required component of surf's reduced-sequence design.
- PosA-VLA anchor failure: what is the downstream effect of incorrect pose prediction on anchor attention? Does incorrect anchoring produce worse task performance than no anchoring (random baseline)? This is the risk surf's structural prior avoids by not requiring explicit pose prediction.
- ElegantVLA composition: when ElegantVLA routes to the lightweight path, does it also reduce the visual token budget automatically, or does the lightweight path still process full visual context? If the lightweight path processes full tokens, combining it with surf's spatial selection could yield a larger compound efficiency gain.
