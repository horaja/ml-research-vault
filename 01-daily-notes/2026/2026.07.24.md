## opener

Friday, July 24. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 18 weeks. GSPA unread — day 41. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 30. Visual Access Boundaries unread — day 10.

**SSI-Policy** (Wang et al.) is the sharpest new structural competitor from today's digest: it builds structured scene interfaces from RGB alone — no depth — for low-data VLA manipulation, achieving "spatial grounding, task-aware reasoning, and precise control." The diagnostic before writing the spec's differentiation section: does SSI-Policy's structured representation co-locate with surf's line-density heatmap on LIBERO contact scenes? If it does, SSI-Policy and surf are selecting the same contact geometry via different structural representations; if it doesn't, surf's line-density prior finds structure SSI-Policy misses. Either way, SSI-Policy is the clearest ablation foil yet for what surf's line prior specifically contributes over a general "structural scene interface."

Two bioRxiv papers today deepen the V4 computational grounding in a new direction. **Cortinovis et al.** show that *action* is a key organizing factor for visual cortex topography — not just shape/texture/animacy — and that topographic deep networks capture this organization. This is the most direct link yet between biological V4 topography and action-relevance: if action co-organizes cortical topology alongside shape, surf's line-drawing prior (which captures geometric contact structure) may be selecting patches aligned with action-organizing cortical features, not just shape-organized ones. The "shape vs. affordance" open question in [[questions]] gets a biological grounding answer here. **Finzi et al.** show dorsal/ventral/lateral stream specialization emerges from a *single* self-supervised objective with a topographic efficiency constraint — no explicit stream labeling needed. If stream specialization is a computational necessity, not a biological accident, surf's prior module has a principled necessity argument that doesn't require the biological V4 literature.

**Dense Embodied Chain-of-Thought** (Li et al.) is the most direct training-time competitor: if dense spatial supervision teaches the VLA what to look at during training, inference-time structural priors may be redundant in a well-supervised system. The go/no-go question is whether dense CoT supervision on LIBERO contact tasks produces a VLA that implicitly identifies contact-geometry regions without explicit prior injection.

Write the spec.
