## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [DepthCache: Depth-Guided Training-Free Visual Token Merging for VLA Inference](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/349a9a94f5d47624a65a84e9125fd430f18f6fea) — Li et al. · 2026
  Uses depth as a structural prior for region-differentiated token merging in VLA — preserves near-field workspace, compresses background. Adds a concrete depth-prior baseline category to [[experiments/e001]] and threatens [[claims/structure helps]] unless line/shape structure beats depth at matched compute.
- [Selective Perception for Robot: Task-Aware Attention in Multimodal VLA](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/ef8997feb9758a7f1bea4eeac1f1a6c27f5a39b6) — Son et al. · 2026
  Lightweight adaptive routing that dynamically fuses multi-view VLA inputs by task. Sits exactly on the prior↔VLA module boundary in [[roadmap]]; directly engages [[questions]] on what makes a patch task-relevant for action and provides a learned-routing baseline against an explicit structural prior.
- [ReconVLA: Reconstructive Vision-Language-Action Model](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/c87ea2afcd9212e5128e01daf17c733d285ac345) — Song et al. · AAAI 2026
  Diffusion-transformer reconstructs the *gaze region* conditioned on VLA visual outputs as an implicit grounding signal. Reframes [[eval/reconstruction]]: reconstruction may be a training objective for attention, not only a relevance metric — relevant before that eval design hardens.

**adjacent**
- [AC^2-VLA: Action-Context-Aware Adaptive Computation in VLA](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/04cf2c44076c84f82673b2ca901f29ec46c7a3e3) — Yu et al. · 2026
  Unified adaptive-compute framework over temporal / spatial / depth redundancies, conditioned on action context. The efficiency/RL module in [[roadmap]] needs to defend its scope against this; in particular, "action context" as the conditioning signal is a direct counter to a value-of-information formulation.
- [LightVLA: Differentiable Token Pruning for VLA](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/6163650edbfba0a837b0722c9c49ed49040ba70d) — Jiang et al. · 2026
  Gumbel-softmax differentiable selector trained end-to-end with the VLA policy. A concrete learned-selector recipe and a baseline-that-could-kill the structural-prior framing; also a candidate template for resolving the selector circular-dependency flagged in [[questions]].
- [Look, Zoom, Understand: The Robotic Eyeball](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/2c0375bf1aaa5637453373a5bd9b267bfdaab26d) — Yang et al. · 2026
  Language-guided active perception: pan/tilt/zoom under explicit pixel/spatial budgets. A foveation-style instantiation of [[concepts/visual budget]] — orthogonal axis to patch-level selection, useful for arguing the budget concept is mechanism-agnostic.

**horizon**
- [The Matching Principle: A Geometric Theory of Loss Functions for Nuisance-Robust Representation Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22800v1) — arXiv 2026-05-21
  Unifies CORAL / IRM / augmentation / Jacobian penalties as encoder-Jacobian regularization along nuisance covariance. Speculative bridge to surf: structural priors as a special case of nuisance suppression where the suppressed nuisance is texture/background — could give a principled story for *why* shape/line works, but only if the nuisance covariance is operationally specified.
- [Enhancing Gaze Reasoning in Vision Foundation Models for Gaze Following](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22607v1) — arXiv 2026-05-21
  Argues VFMs improve *scene understanding* but contribute little to *gaze reasoning*, so they fall back on saliency. Cautionary signal for [[eval/gaze]]: a gaze metric backed by VFM features may grade saliency, not the causal gaze-target relation — exactly the falsifier where "gaze alignment improves while task performance does not" already lives in [[roadmap]].

## questions
- Does line/shape structure beat **depth** structure at matched compute on the same task? If depth wins, the line-prior reframes as "edges as a cheap depth proxy" rather than as evidence for [[claims/structure helps]]. (New, from DepthCache.)
- Does Selective Perception's learned task-aware routing beat a structural-prior selector at matched compute and matched supervision? Combined with yesterday's GuidedVLA question, this is the core empirical threat to [[claims/structure helps]] — implicit prior vs explicit specification vs learned routing.
- Is ReconVLA-style reconstruction-as-grounding doing the same thing as the proposed reconstruction-based relevance metric in [[eval/reconstruction]], or are they orthogonal (objective vs metric)?
- For [[eval/gaze]]: do current VFM-based gaze features grade saliency rather than gaze-causality? If yes, the gaze evaluation may be measuring the wrong thing — preempts the [[roadmap]] falsifier "gaze alignment improves while task performance does not."
- Live from yesterday: does GuidedVLA's explicit task-relevance spec beat a learned structural-prior selector at matched compute?

## risks
- **Three-way threat to [[claims/structure helps]]:** GuidedVLA (explicit spec) + Selective Perception (learned routing) + DepthCache (depth prior). Each is a baseline-that-could-kill on a different axis. Defense needs to be planned, not improvised mid-experiment.
- **[[eval/reconstruction]] design lock-in risk:** ReconVLA suggests reconstruction may be doing grounding work as an objective. Pinning the eval design before this is understood risks measuring the wrong thing.
- **[[eval/gaze]] saliency-confound:** VFM gaze features may grade saliency, not gaze-causality. Worth verifying before any gaze-based experiment claims task relevance.
- **Selector circular dependency:** still open in [[questions]]. LightVLA's Gumbel-softmax recipe is one concrete template — does not resolve the dependency by itself.
- **Biological overclaim:** [[claims/v4 relevance]] must stay framed as motivation, not faithfulness. Recheck phrasing before any external write-up.
