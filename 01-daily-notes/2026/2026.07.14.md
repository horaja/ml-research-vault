## opener

Tuesday, July 14. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 9+ weeks. GSPA unread — day 31. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 20.

Today's digest leads with two papers that cut directly into surf's design assumptions. **"The Ebb and Flow of Multimodal Focus"** (Ye et al., today) provides a mechanistic study of how visual evidence becomes unstable once it enters the VLM language stack — proposing "visual relay windows" as a reinsertion fix during decoding. The relevance to surf is sharp: if visual tokens progressively lose spatial grounding through LLM layers (a collapse STS reportedly also describes), then input-stage token selection before the language stack is architecturally motivated — but the spec must now position surf relative to relay-window reinsertion as a competing repair mechanism. Read this alongside "When Attention Collapses" before writing the novelty section.

**"See like a Robot"** (Lee et al., today) identifies the camera-frame/robot-frame mismatch in VLAs: actions are predicted in the robot's 3D coordinate frame, but all visual observations are camera-frame images. If robot-centric 3D structure is what actually determines action relevance, surf's line-density prior — computed on camera-frame observations — may select 2D-structurally salient patches that misalign with 3D robot-frame contact geometry. The co-location diagnostic: do surf's camera-frame line-dense patches overlap with robot-frame-salient regions (pointmap highlights)?

The spec, STS, GSPA, and Compressor-VLA remain four unresolved critical blockers. Write the spec.
