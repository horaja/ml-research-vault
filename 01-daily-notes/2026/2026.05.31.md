## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [ElegantVLA: Learning When to Think for Efficient Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29438) — Li, Liu et al. · arXiv 2026
  Adaptive compute scheduling for VLA: learns when to invoke heavy reasoning vs. quick-act, rather than running the full model every step at fixed cost. The "when to think" formulation is a near-direct specification of surf's efficiency/RL module — not "how many tokens" but "whether to reason further." Distinct from BRPO (5/30, which uses budget-relative RL reward for anytime LLM reasoning) — this is VLA-specific and frames the decision as a temporal mode switch rather than a continuous budget. Compare ElegantVLA, VLA-ATTC, and BRPO before committing to a stopping-criterion formulation.

- [VLA-ATTC: Adaptive Test-Time Compute for VLA Models with Relative Action Critic Model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01194) — Li, Su et al. · arXiv 2026
  Uses a learned critic to assess action confidence and trigger deliberative reasoning when needed; 5 citations in ~4 weeks. Concrete critic-based implementation of the "when to compute more" decision — most directly comparable to surf's efficiency/RL module. Key question: is the critic trained jointly with the VLA or separately? If separately, it raises the same circular-dependency concern as the surf selector (critic reliability depends on the same features that triggered its invocation).

- [GeoWeaver: Grounding Visual Tokens with Geometric Evidence before Scene Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22558) — Miao, Huang et al. · arXiv 2026
  Argues geometry should be a *prerequisite representational foundation* for spatial reasoning, not a late-fusion auxiliary signal — demonstrates greatest benefit when geometric information is integrated upstream into the visual representation before any reasoning. This is the structural answer to DynaFLIP's dynamics-axis concern: where DynaFLIP argues dynamics pretraining is required for action relevance, GeoWeaver argues geometric structure is the prior that spatial reasoning depends on. Supports [[claims/structure helps]] — line/shape prior as upstream encoder foundation, not a weak annotation.

**adjacent**
- [GuidedVLA: Specifying Task-Relevant Factors via Plug-and-Play Action Attention Specialization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12369) — Jia, Yang et al. · arXiv 2026
  Explicitly specifies which spatial factors matter per task via supervised action attention, rather than relying on end-to-end implicit learning; plug-and-play without full retraining. Baseline threat to [[claims/language helps]]: if explicit attention supervision already fixes spurious correlations without contrastive training, the surf contrastive alignment objective needs to answer what implicit language-contrastive training adds over explicit task-relevant supervision. Stronger baseline than CogVLA (implicit routing) because GuidedVLA directly supervises the attention map.

- [Qwen-VLA: Unifying Vision-Language-Action Modeling across Tasks, Environments, and Robot Embodiments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30280) — Wang, Li et al. · arXiv 2026
  Major unified VLA from Alibaba/Qwen team: single model across manipulation, navigation, and diverse embodiments. Sets the capability frontier for what a general-purpose VLA currently achieves; surf's token-pruning argument must operate on top of (not instead of) models at this capability level. Field-level paper that will be widely referenced.

- [EXPO-FT: Sample-Efficient Reinforcement Learning Finetuning for Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25477) — Dong, Hung, Gao, Sadigh, Finn · arXiv 2026
  From tracked authors Chelsea Finn + Dorsa Sadigh: sample-efficient RL finetuning to close the reliability gap in pretrained VLAs. Relevant to surf's VLA adaptation module — if RL finetuning on top of a pretrained VLA is now sample-efficient, surf's efficiency gain from token pruning can be evaluated on top of RL-finetuned policies rather than pretrained-only baselines, which changes the evaluation setup.

**horizon**
- [Text-Conditional JEPA for Learning Semantically Rich Visual Representations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03245) — Huang, Li et al. (Apple Research) · arXiv 2026
  Uses image captions to reduce feature-prediction ambiguity in I-JEPA, producing richer semantic visual representations under masked prediction. Relevant to the contrastive alignment experiment design: if language supervision during self-supervised pretraining (rather than at inference time) produces more semantically discriminative visual features, surf's contrastive selector may be more effective operating on TC-JEPA features than on CLIP features. Raises the question of whether language-visual alignment should happen at pretraining or fine-tuning.

- [FineVLA: Fine-Grained Instruction Alignment for Steerable Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27284) — Hu, Huang et al. · arXiv 2026
  Fine-grained execution-level language annotations (active arm, approach direction, contact region) rather than coarse goal-level instructions; shows that instruction specificity substantially improves steerability. Directly relevant to surf's language conditioning hypothesis: if fine-grained spatial annotations improve policy steerability, surf's patch scorer operating on coarse-instruction embeddings may be leaving most of the language-condition signal on the table. The granularity of language supervision matters for the [[claims/language helps]] design.

## questions
- VLA-ATTC's relative action critic: is it trained jointly with the VLA (same task distribution, same trajectory data) or as a separate post-hoc critic? If jointly, the critic reliability depends on whether the VLA's confidence is calibrated — which is not guaranteed. If separately, does it generalize across tasks or is it task-specific? The answer determines whether it's an architecture or a fine-tuning recipe.
- ElegantVLA's "when to think" decision: what is the conditioning signal — visual complexity, action prediction uncertainty, task instruction specificity, or something else? If it's instruction-conditioned, it overlaps with surf's language-conditioned selection; if uncertainty-based, it maps onto BRPO's budget-relative reward more directly.
- GuidedVLA's action attention specialization: is the attention supervision at the image-level (which objects matter) or at the patch-level (which regions within an object matter)? Patch-level specialization is a direct competitor; image-level is a different scope.
- GeoWeaver vs. DynaFLIP: GeoWeaver argues geometry is the prerequisite foundation; DynaFLIP argues dynamics pretraining is required for action relevance. Are these two orthogonal claims (structure shapes spatial reasoning; dynamics shapes action relevance) or competing claims (one is sufficient)?
- Carried from 5/30: CogVLA routing mechanism (text embedding vs. cross-attention); DynaFLIP proprioceptive signal conflation; SpecPrune-VLA global history (learned vs. fixed heuristic).
