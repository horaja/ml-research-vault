## opener

Monday, July 13. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 9 weeks. GSPA unread — day 30. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 19.

Today's digest leads with two papers that sharpen the spec's open questions. **Flatness Preserves Instruction Following** (Zhang & Bisk, 2026-06-22) has been on the risks list as "required ablation baseline" for 9 days — surfaced today with a URL. The finding: standard VLA fine-tuning causes instruction blindness by corrupting pretrained representations; flat-minima fine-tuning (SAM-style) repairs it with no structural prior or contrastive objective. The spec diagnostic: does flatness alone recover the contact-region tokens surf's line prior selects? If yes, surf's structural+contrastive approach adds no independent signal over a flatter training objective. If no, the structural prior's value is orthogonal to flatness and the ablation table needs a flatness-controlled baseline. Read this before writing the ablation design section.

**Foveated Dynamic Transformer** (Akkaya et al., arXiv today) integrates eccentricity-based resolution gradients (biological foveation) into ViT for dynamic token selection. New competing design: FDT uses spatial frequency falloff, surf uses structural line density. On manipulation contact regions, do FDT's fovea-centered tokens co-locate with surf's line-dense tokens? If yes, foveation and structure are proxies for the same criterion. If no, surf's prior captures a distinct signal.

**Seeing is Free, Speaking is Not** (Zhan et al., arXiv today) is the first systematic edge-device energy profiling of VLMs. Finding: the language decoder dominates energy cost, not the vision encoder. If reducing visual token count barely affects energy, surf's efficiency argument must shift to latency (attention cost scales quadratically with tokens) and model capacity (fewer visual tokens leave space for reasoning). Read before writing the efficiency motivation in the spec.

The spec, STS, GSPA, and Compressor-VLA are the four critical unresolved blockers. Write the spec.
