Monday, July 20. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 14 weeks. GSPA unread — day 37. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 26. Visual Access Boundaries unread — day 6.

Three papers in today's digest are worth reading before the spec. **"An Exam for Active Observers"** (Zhang et al., arXiv) benchmarks whether MLLMs engage in closed-loop, hypothesis-driven gaze redirection or process a static snapshot. Current MLLMs are passive. The benchmark formalizes exactly the capability gap that [[claims/iterative selection]] is meant to fill architecturally — and gives surf a third evaluation axis alongside [[eval/gaze]] and [[eval/reconstruction]].

**"Data and Learning Where it Matters for Contact-Rich Manipulation"** (Hausdörfer et al., arXiv) provides the data-centric validation surf's structural claim has been missing: contact-rich task performance depends on dense data collection exactly at contact segments; free-space motion tolerates planning-level sparsity. If learning is data-hungry where structural features concentrate, surf's line-density prior selects the informationally dense patches. Read before [[experiments/contrastive alignment]] evaluation design.

**TARL** (Luo et al., ICARS) is the closest new competitor to [[claims/language helps]]: a learnable module adapting frozen VLM visual-language relevancy to manipulation objects via RGB-D + paraphrase consistency regularization, no structural geometric prior. Differentiation needed before writing the spec: what does surf's line-density contrastive signal add over a well-tuned VLM relevancy adapter?

Write the spec.
