## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [Look, Focus, Act: Efficient and Robust Robot Learning via Human Gaze and Foveated Vision Transformers](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/8f7985729b7c0f0f365839dcfcf0474d5a9f6c01) — Chuang et al. · arXiv Jul 2025
  Gaze-guided foveation for robot manipulation: high-res processing at the gaze region, coarse periphery. Adds a concrete gaze-prior baseline to [[experiments/e001]] orthogonal to depth-prior; also directly tests whether gaze-supervision improves task performance (not just saliency alignment) — preempts the [[eval/gaze]] falsifier in [[roadmap]].

- [Active-O3: Empowering Multimodal Large Language Models with Active Perception via GRPO](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/7e40accfc7ae38cea9daa21ff958cedaa7140830) — Zhu et al. · arXiv May 2025
  RL-trained (GRPO) policy for question-conditioned crop selection in MLLMs — the model learns where to look to answer a question. The closest instantiation of the active-perception direction Dr. Lee described; directly engages the [[questions]] comparison between surf's patch-level allocation and Tianqin's question-conditioned framing. Required reading before talking to Tianqin.

- [CogVLA: Cognition-Aligned VLA Model via Instruction-Driven Routing & Sparsification](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/e30e7b2e70556078190f63ac50f613a2f1efd6b3) — Li et al. · arXiv Aug 2025
  Instruction-conditioned token routing + sparse processing in a VLA pipeline. Establishes that language-guided sparsification works at scale in the VLA setting — a published baseline that [[claims/language helps]] must be compared against; also a concrete recipe for the selector circular-dependency question in [[questions]].

**adjacent**
- [SpecPrune-VLA: Accelerating VLA Models via Action-Aware Self-Speculative Pruning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/e5e4c5f50fef44a34d1235659e2cdb6cf3db5a6b) — Wang et al. · arXiv Sep 2025
  Action context drives speculative token pruning across timesteps, not just within-step spatial pruning. The efficiency/RL module in [[roadmap]] needs to distinguish its adaptive allocation from this action-conditioned temporal pruning; useful contrast for scoping the module's novelty claim.

- [Beyond Static Visual Tokens: Structured Sequential Visual Chain-of-Thought Reasoning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/65abecac3d12f5638cd4cc1b966a47bf29f679a4) — Guo et al. · arXiv Mar 2026
  Saliency-gated sequential visual access: the model attends to the most informative region first, then secondary cues. A working coarse-to-fine visual access pattern directly relevant to [[claims/iterative selection]] — tests whether sequential refinement improves reasoning over a single static visual encoding.

- [Good Token Hunting: A Hitchhiker's Guide to Token Selection for Visual Geometry Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23892v1) — Zheng et al. · arXiv 2026-05-25
  Survey and empirical analysis of token selection strategies for multi-view 3D reconstruction. Orthogonal domain but the design-space analysis (what pruning criteria transfer vs fail) could inform the [[experiments/e001]] baseline design and prevent premature commitment to a single selection heuristic.

**horizon**
- [Vision Transformers Need Better Token Interaction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23868v1) — Su et al. · arXiv 2026-05-25
  ViT patch representations degrade for dense prediction during prolonged training — explains the failure as insufficient local token interaction, not just high-norm artifacts. Relevant to surf's assumption that patch-level features are independently useful for selection; if selectivity erodes spatial discriminability, the prior module and the selector may need explicit local-interaction modules.

- [Not Too Generative, Not Too Discriminative: The Human Alignment Sweet Spot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23819v1) — Chang Ortega et al. · arXiv 2026-05-25
  Isolating the learning objective (generative vs discriminative) from architecture/scale shows the alignment with human vision peaks at an intermediate point. Relevant to the [[eval/reconstruction]] design choice: a purely discriminative patch scorer vs reconstruction-based relevance may both fall outside the human-aligned regime that makes task relevance easier to learn.

## questions
- Active-O3 uses GRPO to train the active-perception policy: does the RL reward directly supervise task-relevant crop selection, or does it proxy through answer accuracy? If the latter, surf's structural prior may shortcut the RL training problem — worth specifying before designing the contrastive-alignment experiment.
- Look, Focus, Act's gaze-foveated ViT improves manipulation: is the gain from the foveation architecture, the gaze supervision, or both? Disentangling this matters for whether gaze is a usable structural prior (like line structure) or just a supervision signal.
- CogVLA's instruction routing: is the routing score learned end-to-end with the action loss, or pre-computed from the instruction independently? If end-to-end, it inherits the selector circular-dependency problem in [[questions]] — useful whether it solves it or not.
- From yesterday (live): Is ReconVLA-style reconstruction doing the same grounding work as the proposed [[eval/reconstruction]] metric? Does depth-prior beat line-structure at matched compute?
