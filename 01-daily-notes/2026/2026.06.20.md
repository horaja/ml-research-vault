## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [When Attention Collapses: Stage-Aware Visual Token Pruning from Structure to Semantics](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/63620ae17342d270cc0e01328de8f15726f38637) — Wang, Zhang et al. · Semantic Scholar (Jun 2, 2026)
  The STS paper finally in digest — carried in [[questions]] since 6/16. Core finding: high attention scores collapse onto semantically similar regions, discarding structural diversity. Fix: two-stage pruning — repulsion-based structural sampling first (maximize spatial/geometric coverage), instruction-aware cross-attention filtering second (remove prompt-irrelevant tokens). This is a mechanism-level validation of surf's case for structural priors over attention-only scoring. Key question: does STS's repulsion-based first stage select the same patches as surf's line prior, or do they diverge on texture-heavy scenes? If they converge, surf's line prior may be providing a hand-crafted version of repulsion-based structural coverage.

- [CogVLA: Cognition-Aligned Vision-Language-Action Model via Instruction-Driven Routing & Sparsification](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/e30e7b2e70556078190f63ac50f613a2f1efd6b3) — Li, Zhang, Shao et al. · arXiv (Aug 2025)
  Instruction-conditioned routing and sparsification for VLA without contrastive training or geometric priors. A new direct threat to [[claims/language helps]]: CogVLA achieves language-conditioned visual sparsification purely through instruction embedding, which may subsume what surf's contrastive scorer adds. Surf's differentiation must be specific — does the geometric structural prior add over instruction routing alone? Does end-to-end contrastive training improve over soft routing? Not previously surfaced.

- [PosPrune: Visual Token Pruning with Positional Bias Correction for Efficient Large Vision-Language Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/608a7b556b1a5dad9371eae6afea8f8675028783) — Wang, Li, Yin et al. · AAAI 2026 (Mar 2026)
  Explicitly measures and corrects for center/positional bias in VLM token pruning — existing methods select central tokens preferentially due to positional correlates in CLS attention. Direct relevance to surf's "center bias in line-guided selection" carried risk: PosPrune is the natural comparison point for demonstrating that surf's structural prior selects beyond what a positional-bias-corrected baseline would pick. If PosPrune's corrected selection matches surf's line-prior selection, surf's added cost is unjustified.

**adjacent**
- [EventVLA: Event-Driven Visual Evidence Memory for Long-Horizon Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20092v1) — Yang, Tu et al. · arXiv (Jun 18, 2026)
  Event-triggered gating for deciding which visual cues to retain across long-horizon VLA rollouts — discards irrelevant history, stores task-relevant "events." An alternative architectural answer to surf's selective visual evidence question, operating at temporal (between-step) granularity rather than spatial (within-frame patch) granularity. Relevant to [[claims/iterative selection]]: the event-trigger definition is a coarser-granularity version of surf's when-to-stop criterion — comparing the two would sharpen what "task relevance" means at different timescales.

- [Fast Human Attention Prediction for Fixation-guided Active Perception in Autonomous Navigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20491v1) — Mohammed, Malczyk, Alexis et al. · arXiv (Jun 18, 2026)
  GazeLNN: lightweight scanpath prediction model (Liquid Neural Networks) for real-time fixation-guided robot active perception. Relevant to [[eval/gaze]]: if a computationally cheap scanpath model trained on human gaze data can guide robot visual attention, it competes with surf's contrastive scorer as a gaze-supervision alternative. Key question: is GazeLNN's scanpath trained on navigation/manipulation-specific gaze, or generic scene viewing? Task-specific gaze would directly overlap with surf's target; generic gaze would not.

- [Finetuning Vision-Language-Action Models Requires Fewer Layers Than You Think](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20246v1) — Nguyen, Ho et al. · arXiv (Jun 18, 2026)
  Empirically identifies that VLA fine-tuning performance is dominated by a small subset of layers — most of the VLA is frozen-equivalent during adaptation. Directly informs surf's open question from 6/19 (One Layer's Trash) about which ViT/LLM layer to apply the contrastive scorer at: if only a few layers drive manipulation behavior, contrastive scoring should be applied at those layers. If those layers are also the ones affected by semantic diffusion (Su et al., 6/18), this converges on a specific layer-depth recommendation.

**horizon**
- [Visual Instruction Tuning Aligns Modalities through Abstraction](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/397d7e7accda79a898ef8a9a8653c01b7a37affe) — Palacios, Basile et al. · Semantic Scholar (Jun 2, 2026)
  Instruction tuning embeds visual features into intermediate semantic layers of the LLM backbone, bypassing early layers — "multimodal integration is a localized phenomenon." Directly relevant to the layer-depth question: surf's contrastive scorer should extract selection signals from the intermediate LLM layers where visual-language alignment actually occurs, not from early visual layers or final LLM layers. Pairs with "Finetuning Fewer Layers" and "One Layer's Trash" (6/19) as a third convergent data point on where surf's scorer should operate.

- [Sparsity, Superposition, and Forgetting: A Mechanistic Study of Representation Retention in Continual Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20431v1) — Wasilewski, Kozal et al. · arXiv (Jun 18, 2026)
  Superposition (directional feature overlap) increases with continued training, with transient dips at task boundaries; sparse features induce more superposition but don't inevitably cause forgetting if representations remain strong. Horizon connection: VLA fine-tuning is a form of continual learning that introduces manipulation supervision over a pretrained VLM. If superposition increases through this fine-tuning, patches whose features are underrepresented in the manipulation distribution may lose representation strength — the structurally-informative patches that surf's line prior targets. This would explain why geometric priors might be necessary: they explicitly surface representations that VLA fine-tuning's implicit superposition would compress away.

## questions

- STS structural stage mechanism: does STS's repulsion-based first stage select tokens by maximizing pairwise distance in feature space, or by spatial grid coverage? If feature-space repulsion, does it select the same patches as surf's line prior on manipulation scenes with geometric structure? If they converge, surf's line prior is a cheap proxy for repulsion-based structural coverage.
- CogVLA routing mechanism: does CogVLA's instruction-driven routing use a learned gating function over instruction tokens, or does it embed the full instruction into a routing policy? Does it require task-specific routing training, or does routing emerge from VLM supervision? If it emerges from pretrained VLM supervision, the bar for surf's language conditioning is higher than expected.
- PosPrune bias correction: what is PosPrune's positional bias correction — a learned position prior subtracted from attention, or a spatial re-weighting? How large is the gap between corrected and uncorrected selection on manipulation-relevant scenes? If gap is small, center bias may not be the dominant confound in surf's line-prior results.
- EventVLA event trigger: what counts as an "event" in EventVLA — object occlusion, contact, scene change, or instruction phase transition? If phase transition, EventVLA's event trigger is a coarser version of When Does Language Matter?'s language-sensitive step — and could be used to gate surf's step-adaptive contrastive scorer.
- GazeLNN training data: is GazeLNN's Liquid NN scanpath model trained on human gaze from robot navigation, from general scene viewing, or from driving? Task-specificity determines whether GazeLNN's scanpath correlates with manipulation-relevant patch selection.
- Finetuning fewer layers: does the "fewer layers needed" finding hold for continuous-control VLAs (pi_0 family) specifically? And do the critical layers differ between pick-and-place and contact-rich manipulation? If contact-rich tasks require different layers, surf's contrastive scorer may need task-conditioned layer selection.
