### digest (auto)

**core**
- [VisionPulse: Dynamic Visual Sparsity for Efficient Multimodal Reasoning](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/8ca56d80ff32ff046efa7012deaafff1091c470d) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Shows that only a sparse, evolving subset of visual tokens is critical at each decoding step, and that redundant visual context steers the model toward query-irrelevant regions, lengthening reasoning. If this step-dependency holds in VLA closed-loop inference (not just chain-of-thought reasoning), surf's current one-shot patch selection at observation time is the wrong architecture — [[claims/iterative selection]] needs to be designed first, not deferred.

- [DiffPrune: Fully Differentiable Token Pruning for Vision-Language Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/5865585d29f0c9c83b717729ec3f82edc705bed4) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Replaces Gumbel-Softmax surrogate gradients with an Information Throttler that modulates each token using variance-preserving noise conditioned on importance scores — pruning becomes continuous information suppression rather than discrete selection during training. Directly relevant to surf's selector training: if the circular-dependency problem (selector inputs co-trained with selected features) is exacerbated by discrete selection, DiffPrune's continuous formulation is a cleaner training path. Read before committing to a selector training objective.

- [RoboSemanticBench: Diagnosing Semantic Grounding in Action Prediction for VLA Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02277v1) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Benchmark specifically designed to catch shortcut exploitation in VLA fine-tuning; finds that VLAs can achieve high task success via visual/instruction-action shortcuts without genuine semantic grounding of the language instruction. Directly relevant to surf's eval design: if VLAs already solve tasks via shortcuts, token pruning that preserves those shortcut features looks good on task success but measures nothing about semantic grounding. Surf's eval needs a shortcut-controlled benchmark; RSB may be it alongside [[behavior env stanford]].

**adjacent**
- [Action with Visual Primitives](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/4b577d036e78bcf916afadceade697bacc57b0ad) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Decomposes the single-pass VLA forward (instruction + visual → action) into explicit instruction comprehension, spatial scene understanding, and motor control modules, arguing that entanglement of these objectives forces the action module to relearn perceptual capabilities already present in the VLM. The spatial scene understanding module is a direct analog of surf's prior module; the decomposition argument supports surf's three-module framing at the level of learning objective design, not just architecture.

- [Event-Grounded Sparse Autoencoders for Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1b1a40c218d97bb68f8ebea2ad7f5ca52f1347c4) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Mechanistic interpretability for VLAs: SAE features are anchored to behavioral events (end-effector keyframes) rather than text contexts, identifying which hidden representations drive action. Relevant to surf's motivation: if specific VLA representations are known to drive action, surf's patch selection can be evaluated against whether it preserves those representation-active regions, not just downstream task success. Potential methodological contribution for the [[eval]] design.

- [AHEAD: Intercepting the Future — Latent-Space Predictive World Model for Dynamic VLA Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02486v1) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Wraps a frozen VLA with a motion-aware latent world model that forecasts future patch tokens before acting; closes the latency gap when objects move during task execution. Introduces a new failure axis for surf: within-frame static patch selection ignores scene dynamics, and if the selection happens on a stale observation while the scene has changed, the selection is wrong regardless of prior quality. Note whether AHEAD's world model trains on the same visual representation as the VLA — if so, its fine-tuning affects the feature distribution surf's structural prior was designed to select.

**horizon**
- [EAPO: Learning When Not to Act — Mitigating Tool Abuse in Agentic RL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02132v1) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Difficulty-aware reward shaping penalizes redundant tool calls on easy queries, teaching agents to solve problems internally when possible. Direct horizon analog to surf's stopping criterion: the efficiency/RL module must learn when NOT to request additional patches, not only when to request them. EAPO's difficulty-aware conservative penalty is a concrete RL algorithm for the "stop requesting" design — compare against BRPO (budget-relative reward) before committing to a stopping-criterion formulation.

- [VISReg: Variance-Invariance-Sketching Regularization for JEPA training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02572v1) — Unnamed et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Prevents embedding collapse in JEPA training by aligning embeddings to distributional shape (not just second-order statistics), combining VICReg's variance/covariance objectives with sketching-based isotropic alignment. Horizon connection: TC-JEPA (5/31 horizon) was flagged as a candidate feature space for surf's contrastive selector; VISReg addresses the training instability that makes JEPA features less reliable. If surf evaluates TC-JEPA features, VISReg-trained variants are the quality ceiling.

## questions
- VisionPulse step-dependency: does the evolving critical token set reflect changing question context across reasoning steps (specific to chain-of-thought), or does it also apply to VLA closed-loop inference where the instruction is fixed but the scene evolves? The answer determines whether surf needs iterative selection (new design) or whether one-shot is sufficient for VLA.
- AHEAD world model: does the motion-aware latent world model train on the same visual representation as the frozen VLA, or a separate motion encoder trained from scratch? If shared, AHEAD fine-tuning shifts the feature distribution that surf's structural prior selects. If separate, the two are orthogonal and AHEAD could be composed with surf.
- DiffPrune's Information Throttler: does the continuous information suppression during training produce different pruning patterns than Gumbel-Softmax at inference? The training objective is different but the inference behavior (binary pruning) may converge to the same solution — or it may produce softer masking, which changes the efficiency guarantee.
