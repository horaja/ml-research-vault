# 2026-07-06

## opener

Monday, July 6. GSPA unread — day 23. "When Attention Collapses" (STS) unread — day 12. Contrastive alignment spec unwritten — 7+ weeks.

Today's lead: DeepGaze3.5-VL (Agrawal, Bethge, Kümmerer; arXiv 2607.02083) replaces hand-crafted scanpath priors with a VLM backbone and autoregressive fixation token prediction, conditioning on scene content, task description, and prior fixations. This is the "DAA complement/replacement" candidate on the reading list. The measurement surf needs: compute IoU between surf's top-k patch mask and DeepGaze3.5-VL predicted fixation locations on LIBERO manipulation scenes. If IoU > random baseline, the structural prior is aligned with human attention. If not, the divergence is informative — surf's line prior may select geometrically dense contact zones that humans don't fixate on, which may still be action-relevant but would falsify a gaze-based validation argument. This distinguishes the two [[eval/gaze]] hypotheses: "gaze tracks task relevance" vs. "gaze tracks appearance saliency."

Second: Zhou et al. (arXiv 2607.01987) apply causal subspace interventions to probe how SSL ViTs encode dense geometric information. They decompose linear probe weights to isolate which feature subspaces carry depth, surface normals, and edge information. Relevant to [[claims/structure helps]]: if surf-selected patches have higher geometric subspace scores than randomly-selected patches, the structural prior claim gains a mechanistic grounding — it's not just that lines co-occur with task-relevant objects, but that selected patches encode the geometric information that disambiguates affordances. Also provides a partial answer to the textual-noise-dispersion problem (EADP, 7/3): if the contrastive scorer is restricted to geometrically informative subspaces, the language signal competes in a smaller, more structured space.
