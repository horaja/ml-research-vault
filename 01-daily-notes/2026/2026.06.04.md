## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [When Attention Collapses: Stage-Aware Visual Token Pruning from Structure to Semantics](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/63620ae17342d270cc0e01328de8f15726f38637) — Wang et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Argues that using a single attention-score metric for pruning causes collapse onto semantically similar regions, discarding structural diversity. Proposes stage-aware pruning: early layers use structural signals, later layers use semantic signals. Architecture-level support for surf's line-guided prior being applied at the right network stage — and a constraint: if the structural stage window is early and narrow, surf's selector needs to operate there, not after semantic features have collapsed diversity.

- [AdaMerge: Salience-Aware Adaptive Token Merging for Training-Free Acceleration of Vision Transformers](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1e3956fe689c5e973f631847ebe904e10d2ef77c) — Lee et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Extends ToMe by relaxing the token-equality assumption: protects high-salience tokens from aggressive merging while merging low-salience ones. Training-free. Closest baseline for [[claims/structure helps]]: if salience-aware merging without any structural prior achieves comparable task performance, the structural prior's contribution becomes the variable to isolate. What does surf's line-guided selection add over just knowing which tokens are salient?

- [Visual Instruction Tuning Aligns Modalities through Abstraction](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/397d7e7accda79a898ef8a9a8653c01b7a37affe) — Palacios et al. · arXiv Jun 2026
  Shows, across diverse VLM architectures, that instruction tuning embeds visual features into the intermediate semantic layers of the LLM backbone — not early layers, not late layers. Multimodal integration is a localized, layer-specific phenomenon that repurposes the LLM's existing abstraction machinery. Design constraint for [[experiments/contrastive alignment]]: the contrastive alignment signal for surf's selector should be extracted from the identified intermediate abstraction layers, not a pooled or last-layer representation.

**adjacent**
- [Qwen-VLA: Unifying Vision-Language-Action Modeling across Tasks, Environments, and Robot Embodiments](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1636ab3d193c8effeca613fde281e49ac453c8f8) — Wang et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Unified embodied foundation model extending Qwen's VL stack to action; addresses fragmented per-task VLA specialization by training across heterogeneous embodied tasks. Current SOTA unified VLA — relevant as the architectural baseline surf's efficiency and selection modules would need to be composable with. Note scale and training data scope before assuming surf can attach to it directly.

- [Vision Transformers Need Better Token Interaction](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/ce02cd3ecf85e6f7872055908017b00391534a0d) — Su · arXiv May 2026
  Identifies "semantic diffusion" — an optimization shortcut in ViT where global semantic information propagates into patch tokens beyond local justification, causing dense prediction to degrade. Third entry in the representation-distortion thread (RESTORE 6/3, PosPrune 6/1, now semantic diffusion). Implication for surf: line-guided structural selection is most reliable before semantic diffusion has spread global semantics into local patch tokens — early in the network and early in training.

- [Good Token Hunting: A Hitchhiker's Guide to Token Selection for Visual Geometry Transformers](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/369107734d2b246ad7006802394d641a6592150a) — Zheng et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Studies token selection for multi-view 3D reconstruction: restricts key/value tokens per query to preserve geometric accuracy while reducing quadratic attention cost. Geometry-grounded selection reference case for surf: in reconstruction, geometric tokens outperform semantic ones — if the same holds in VLA manipulation (where 3D geometry determines contact regions), surf's structural prior is the right selection criterion. Cross-domain argument to include in related work.

**horizon**
- [MergeTok: Unified Continuous and Discrete Visual Tokenization via Token Merging](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/b51ab44d27734c73b85e861f68fa223867d48702) — Zhang et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Bridges continuous VAE and discrete VQ tokenization in one architecture via token merging, jointly optimizing both objectives. Horizon implication: if the visual representation surf's selector operates on transitions to unified continuous/discrete tokens, the selection problem changes structurally — merging has already partially performed spatial aggregation, and the residual selection problem is different from selecting among raw patch tokens.

- [EXPO-FT: Sample-Efficient Reinforcement Learning Finetuning for Vision-Language-Action Models](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/268947e1530cf5bd29563874af2253cd68df1f79) — Dong, Hung, Gao, **Sadigh, Finn** et al. · arXiv May 2026
  Sample-efficient RL finetuning of VLA policies that leverages pretrained priors rather than RL from scratch; addresses the reliability gap between pretrained VLA performance and real-world deployment requirements. Directly relevant to surf's efficiency/RL module: if RL finetuning of a pretrained VLA can be sample-efficient when the pretrained policy provides a good prior, the adaptive compute policy may be trainable with less RL data than assumed.
