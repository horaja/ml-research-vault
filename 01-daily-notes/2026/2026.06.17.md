## papers

### digest (auto)

**core**
- [See First, Answer Later: Visual Evidence Pre-Alignment via Sufficiency-Driven RL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17678v1) — Liu, Wang et al. · arXiv
  Trains MLLMs via RL to identify which visual regions suffice for answering before committing to a response. The RL objective directly operationalizes "visual sufficiency" — the exact framing surf needs for its contrastive scorer and iterative selection stopping criterion. This is surf's core thesis implemented for MLLMs without geometric priors or VLA action heads; it is simultaneously a baseline to beat and a validation that the RL-for-visual-sufficiency framing is tractable. Read before spec is finalized.

- [PearlVLA: Progressive Embodied Action-Plan Refinement in Latent Space](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17924v1) — Yang, Zhao et al. · arXiv
  Addresses VLA efficiency-vs-deliberation tradeoff via progressive latent-space action refinement — implicit multi-step reasoning without explicit chain-of-thought overhead. Relevant to [[claims/iterative selection]]: latent-space iterative refinement of action plans is architecturally analogous to surf's coarse-to-fine visual selection, but operating on action representations rather than visual tokens. The key question: if latent-space plan refinement subsumes the need for visual token refinement, what does surf's visual-level iteration add?

- [ThinkingVLA: Interleaved Vision and Language Reasoning for Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17937v1) — Lu, Chen et al. · arXiv
  Adds explicit interleaved vision-language reasoning (CoT) to VLA manipulation, enabling subgoal decomposition and spatial anticipation without sacrificing action generation speed. Adjacent to surf's contrastive alignment design: if reasoning steps are interleaved with visual processing, each reasoning step could in principle gate which patches are selected next — making ThinkingVLA an architectural substrate for surf's language-conditioned selective attention. The open question: does ThinkingVLA's spatial anticipation improve by conditioning on fewer but more relevant visual patches, or does it require dense visual context for reliable reasoning?

**adjacent**
- [Visual Verification Enables Inference-time Steering and Autonomous Policy Improvement (VERITAS)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18247v1) — Zhang, Agrawal et al. · arXiv
  Generator-verifier framework for robot policies: a verifier scores candidate policy rollouts and steers inference toward higher-quality actions without retraining. Related to [[claims/iterative selection]] from a different angle — the verifier is a form of selective attention to which robot actions are worth executing, while surf's selector attends to which visual tokens are worth processing. If a visual verifier is separable from an action verifier, the two could compose.

- [Qwen-RobotManip Technical Report: Alignment Unlocks Scale for Robotic Manipulation Foundation Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17846v1) — Yuan, Li et al. · arXiv
  Large-scale robot manipulation foundation model showing that heterogeneous data alignment (not just scale) is the key ingredient for cross-scene manipulation generalization. Industry-scale signal on where manipulation foundation model investment is concentrated — and confirmation that alignment over heterogeneous data is a live research axis alongside visual efficiency.

- [EBench: Elemental Diagnosis of Generalist Mobile Manipulation Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18239v1) — Gao et al. · arXiv
  Benchmark decomposing manipulation success into 5 capability dimensions × 4 generalization dimensions, addressing the "single success-rate scalar" evaluation gap. Relevant for surf's evaluation design: if visual token pruning reduces success rate, EBench's decomposition could identify which capability dimension degrades — spatial reasoning? object recognition? generalization to new scenes? — making it a more informative eval than aggregate success rate.

**horizon**
- [Variable-Width Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18246v1) — Wu, Chen et al. · arXiv
  Learns per-layer, per-token width profiles — not all dimensions of every hidden state are equally useful at every layer. Width-adaptive compute is orthogonal to surf's token-count-adaptive approach: fewer tokens vs. narrower representations at each token. Both are under the "adaptive compute budget" umbrella. If the two compose (width-narrow the selected tokens), the efficiency gain multiplies; if they interfere (width adaptation already implicitly selects tokens), surf's token-count selection may overlap.

- [SuCo: Sufficiency-guided Continuous Adaptive Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17687v1) — Wang et al. · arXiv
  Trains LRMs to stop generating chain-of-thought when accumulated reasoning is sufficient, using a continuous sufficiency signal rather than a fixed budget or discrete mode switch. The stopping-criterion design is directly analogous to surf's when-to-stop problem in [[claims/iterative selection]]: SuCo's "sufficiency score" for reasoning steps is a candidate template for surf's "information sufficiency score" for patch selection passes. Horizon because it's in LLM reasoning, not vision or VLA — but the framing transfers.

## questions

- SEE-SAL visual sufficiency: does SEE-SAL's RL objective (identify sufficient regions, then answer) generalize from VQA to instruction-following manipulation? If the sufficiency signal in VQA is answer accuracy, what is the sufficiency signal in VLA — action success? predicted contact point? The answer determines whether SEE-SAL's training recipe transfers to surf.
- PearlVLA refinement axis: does PearlVLA's progressive latent-space refinement reduce the effective compute compared to a single-pass VLA decode, or does it increase compute in exchange for quality? If it increases compute, the iterative refinement axis (visual patch selection vs. action plan refinement) are both buying quality with compute — surf's claim needs to show its visual-level refinement is more efficient or more informative than latent-level refinement.
- ThinkingVLA reasoning-vision coupling: does ThinkingVLA's spatial anticipation step attend uniformly to all patches, or does the CoT generate explicit spatial references that could localize attention? If CoT generates region references, surf's structural prior could replace or augment the CoT spatial anticipation step — making the two composable rather than competing.
